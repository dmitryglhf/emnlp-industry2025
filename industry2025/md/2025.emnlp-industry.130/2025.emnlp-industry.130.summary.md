# Summary

## TL;DR  
AI Knowledge Assist is a cost‑effective, LLaMA‑3.1‑8B‑fine‑tuned system that automatically extracts, deduplicates, and recommends question‑answer (QA) pairs from noisy call transcripts. It achieves **>90 %** accuracy on 20 real‑world Dialpad contact‑center datasets, outperforming larger closed‑source LLMs and enabling immediate deployment of Retrieval‑Augmented Generation (RAG) chatbots.

## Problem & Motivation  
Contact centers lack a dedicated knowledge base, creating a *cold‑start* barrier for conversational AI agents. Building a knowledge base from scratch is time‑consuming and requires annotated data that is hard to obtain in noisy business conversations. The paper addresses this gap by leveraging historical customer‑agent transcripts to automatically populate an FAQ‑style repository.

## Approach  
The system follows a **three‑stage pipeline** (Figure 2):  
1. **Knowledge Extraction** – an LLM is prompted to extract and rewrite information‑seeking questions and their answers from each transcript \(T\) (Equation (1)).  
2. **Clustering for Deduplication** – question embeddings (BGE‑Large) are compared via cosine distance (Equation (2)) and clustered with DBSCAN to group semantically similar QA pairs.  
3. **Recommending Representative QA Pairs** – a second LLM processes each cluster and selects one or more representative QA pairs (Equation (3)), which are then inserted or reviewed by a Knowledge Manager.

The backbone model is **LLaMA‑3.1‑8B** fine‑tuned on 5,500 training transcripts and evaluated on 7,000 transcripts. The annotator used for dataset creation is **Gemini‑2.5‑Pro**.

## Experimental Setup  
| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Dataset** | 20 Dialpad client companies, November 2024, call transcripts (avg 855 words), anonymized via Google Cloud DLP. |
| **Annotation** | 27,500 instances: 12,500 for extraction (5,500 train / 7,000 eval) and 15,000 for recommendation (2,500 train / 12,500 eval). |
| **Baselines** | LLaMA‑3.1‑8B‑Instruct, DeepSeek‑R1‑LLaMA‑8B, GPT‑4o‑Mini, Gemini‑2.0‑Flash, Gemini‑2.0‑Flash‑Lite, Gemini‑2.5‑Flash‑Lite. |
| **Metrics** | Precision, Recall, F1, ROUGE‑1/2/L, BERTScore, Silhouette (for clustering). |
| **Evaluation** | Reference‑free: GPT‑4o judge counts correct QA pairs. |
| **Implementation** | HuggingFace for open‑source, official APIs for closed‑source. 3 epochs, max seq 8000 tokens, learning rate 2e‑4–2e‑6, 8 × A100 GPUs. |

Figure 3 shows that the Knowledge‑Assist‑8B‑SFT achieves **>90 % F1** for most company types.  
Table 3 demonstrates that replacing the backbone with Qwen3‑8B drops F1 to **74.73 %**, and using GPT‑4o instead of Gemini‑2.5‑Pro for annotation reduces F1 to **65.95 %**, highlighting the importance of model choice.

Human evaluation (Table 4) on 100 conversations shows **25 %** preference for Knowledge‑Assist‑8B‑SFT vs **17 %** for Gemini‑2.5‑Flash‑Lite, with **58 %** of responses rated as “Tie”.
