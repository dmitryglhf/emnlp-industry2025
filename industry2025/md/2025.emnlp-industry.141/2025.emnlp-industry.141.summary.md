# Summary

## TL;DR  
LLMInit introduces a lightweight, selective initialization strategy that injects large‑language‑model (LLM) embeddings into collaborative‑filtering (CF) recommender systems. By sampling only 128 dimensions from high‑dimensional LLM embeddings (via random, uniform, or variance‑based selection) and aggregating them for users, LLMInit boosts Recall@10 and NDCG@10 on four Amazon datasets while keeping the parameter count at 2 M, making it a practical, industry‑ready solution for cold‑start and data‑sparse scenarios.

## Problem & Motivation  
Collaborative filtering (CF) models suffer from cold‑start and sparse interaction data. Existing CF approaches learn embeddings from scratch, leading to poor performance when users or items have few interactions. Large language models (LLMs) such as GPT‑4 or LLaMA contain rich semantic knowledge that could help, but deploying full LLMs at scale is infeasible due to their huge computational cost, latency, and token limits. The paper therefore asks: *How can we harness LLMs for RecSys in an effective and scalable manner?* The motivation is to provide an industry‑grade method that leverages LLM knowledge without incurring the full cost of running an LLM for every recommendation query.

## Approach  
LLMInit is a plug‑and‑play framework that injects LLM embeddings into CF models:

1. **LLM embedding generation** – raw metadata (e.g., item titles, descriptions) is concatenated and fed to a pretrained LLM (MPNet, gte‑Qwen, GPT‑S, GPT‑L, Stella). The LLM outputs a high‑dimensional vector **v ∈ ℝⁿ** (n up to 8 096).
2. **Selective item embedding initialization** – only **K = 128** dimensions are kept. Three sampling strategies are proposed:
   * **LLMInit‑Uni** – evenly spaced indices.
   * **LLMInit‑Rand** – random indices.
   * **LLMInit‑Var** – top‑K dimensions with highest variance across the dataset.
3. **User embedding aggregation** – for a user *i* with interacted items *Nᵢ*, the user vector is the mean of the selected item vectors:
   \[
   \mathbf{u}_i = \frac{1}{|N_i|}\sum_{j\in N_i}\mathbf{v}_j .
   \]
4. The resulting 128‑dimensional CF embeddings are then fed into standard CF models (LightGCN, SGL, SGCL) without further modification.

## Experimental Setup  
- **Datasets**: Four real‑world Amazon review sets – Beauty, Toys‑Games, Tools‑Home, Office‑Products (5‑core setting, leave‑one‑out split).  
- **Baselines**:  
  * CF: LightGCN (He et al., 2020), SGL (Wu et al., 2021), SGCL (Zhang et al., 2025b).  
  * LLM‑based: MoRec (Yuan et al., 2023), LLMRank (Hou et al., 2024), LLMRec (Wei et al., 2024), TIGER (Rajput et al., 2023).  
- **Metrics**: Recall@10 and NDCG@10.  
- **Embedding dimension**: Fixed to **K = 128** for all models.  
- **LLM generator**: MPNet (Song et al., 2020) by default.  
- **Parameter counts**:  
  * MoRec, TIGER, LLMInit‑Full – ~13 M–14 M.  
  * LLMRank, LLMRec – 7 B + 13 M.  
  * LLMInit‑Var – **2 M**.

**Key observations**

- LLMInit‑Var consistently yields the best gains across all baselines and datasets, with up to **+12.0 %** Recall@10 on Beauty (LightGCN) and **+8.8 %** on Toys‑Games (SGL).  
- LLMInit‑Var achieves these improvements with only **2 M** parameters, far below the 7 B+13 M of LLMRec or the 7 B of LLMRank.  
- In cold‑start experiments (half of each user’s interactions removed), SGCL with LLMInit‑Var improves Recall and NDCG by **>50.4 %** and **>58.9 %** respectively, showing the method’s strength when interaction data is scarce.
