# Summary

## TL;DR
FQ‑Eval introduces a user‑centered evaluation dataset for follow‑up question generation in chat‑LLM services. It defines five human‑aligned criteria, curates 1,000 high‑quality follow‑up questions from 200 real‑world QA pairs, and demonstrates that existing LLMs (e.g., GPT‑4.1, Claude Opus 4) perform poorly on several criteria, especially *Creative Leap* and *LLM Enablement*.

## Problem & Motivation
Chat‑LLM services are widely used, but users often struggle to express their goals, leading to conversations that diverge from the intended purpose. Existing follow‑up question research focuses mainly on information‑seeking or topical relevance, neglecting how such questions can satisfy users’ intrinsic needs and help achieve conversational goals. A standardized, user‑centered evaluation framework is missing, hindering progress in real‑world chat‑LLM deployments.

## Approach
1. **Task Definition** – Generate one or more follow‑up questions \(f\) given a single‑turn user question \(q\) and its answer \(a\): \(f=\mathcal{F}(q,a)\).  
2. **Human‑aligned Criteria** – Five criteria derived from a multi‑stage user study:  
   * Exploratory Scope (C1)  
   * Contextual Relevance (C2)  
   * Creative Leap (C3)  
   * LLM Enablement (C4)  
   * Guided Onboarding (C5)  
3. **Dataset Construction** –  
   * **Seed Questions**: 100 use cases from a Harvard Business Review (HBR) study → 5 candidate questions per complexity level (simple/complex) → 1,000 candidates. Annotators select and refine one per level → 200 final seed questions.  
   * **Answers**: Generated by GPT‑4.1‑2025‑04‑14, verified by annotators.  
   * **Follow‑up Questions**: For each of the 200 QA pairs, GPT‑4.1‑2025‑04‑14 generates 8 candidates per criterion (40 total). Seven annotators select the best per criterion and revise for clarity, yielding 1,000 curated follow‑up questions (5 per QA pair).  

## Experimental Setup
* **Models Evaluated** – 12 LLMs from four major providers: GPT‑4.1, GPT‑4.1‑mini, GPT‑4.1‑nano, Claude Opus 4, Claude Sonnet 4, Claude 3.5 Haiku, Mistral Large, Mistral Medium, Mistral Small, Gemini 2.5 Flash, etc.  
* **Evaluation Methods** –  
  * **n‑way Selection**: Human evaluators choose the best follow‑up question among the FQ‑Eval representative and model‑generated candidates for each criterion.  
  * **Score‑based Evaluation**: Each candidate receives a 1–5 score per criterion.  
* **Metrics** – Selection rate (%) and raw count, average rank.  
* **Prompt Settings** – GPT‑4.1‑2025‑04‑14, temperature 0.0, top‑p 1.0.
