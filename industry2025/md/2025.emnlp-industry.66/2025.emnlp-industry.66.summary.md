# Summary

## TL;DR
The paper proposes a two‑stage training pipeline—supervised fine‑tuning (SFT) with high‑quality cold‑start data followed by reinforcement learning (RL) using Group Relative Policy Optimization (GRPO)—to improve large vision‑language models for Video Temporal Grounding (VTG). The method, called **VTG‑R1**, consistently outperforms state‑of‑the‑art baselines on three benchmarks (NExTGQA, RexTime, Charades‑STA) with up to 10‑point gains in R@0.3 and 4‑point gains in mIoU.

## Problem & Motivation
VTG requires precise temporal localization of moments in untrimmed videos given natural language queries. Existing LVLM‑based approaches rely heavily on supervised fine‑tuning, which limits temporal awareness and generalization, especially in challenging or open‑domain scenarios. The paper addresses this gap by integrating RL to explicitly reward temporal precision and structured reasoning.

## Approach
1. **Data Curation**  
   - Source data aggregated from 5 moment‑retrieval datasets (HiREST, QuerYD, TACoS, DiDeMo, InternVid‑VTime) and 1 query‑grounding dataset (Grounded‑VLLM).  
   - Two filtered subsets:  
     *TVG‑Coldstart‑13K* (IoU > ε¹=0.8) for SFT,  
     *TVG‑RL‑18K* (IoU < ε²=0.4) for RL.  
2. **Prompt Template** – a system prompt enforcing `<think>` reasoning and `<time>` answer tags.  
3. **SFT Stage** – one epoch of supervised fine‑tuning on *TVG‑Coldstart‑13K* to initialize the policy.  
4. **RL Stage** –  
   - Reward: weighted sum of IoU reward (r_tIoU) and format reward (r_form) with λ_tIoU=0.9, λ_form=0.1.  
   - Algorithm: GRPO (no critic), with KL‑divergence β=0.0.  
   - Training: 600 steps, max response length 2048 tokens.  
   - Model: Qwen2.5‑VL‑7B (base) or 32B variant.

## Experimental Setup
| Item | Details |
|------|----------|
| Benchmarks | NExTGQA, RexTime, Charades‑STA |
| Metrics | R@0.3, R@0.5, R@0.7, mIoU |
| Baselines | VTimeLLM, TimeChat, VideoChat‑TPO, Qwen2.5‑VL‑7B, Qwen2.5‑VL‑32B |
| Hardware | 16 × NVIDIA H100 (80 GB) |
| Video | 64 frames, 128 × 28 × 28 resolution |
| Hyper‑params | ε₁=0.8, ε₂=0.4, λ_tIoU=0.9, λ_form=0.1, β=0.0 |

## Results
| Model | R@0.3 | R@0.5 | R@0.7 | mIoU |
|-------|--------|--------|--------|------|
| **VTG‑R1** (NExTGQA) | **41.65** | **20.78** | **10.01** | **29.25** |
| VTimeLLM | 37.90 | 20.20 | 9.71 | 24.40 |
| VideoChat‑TPO | 41.20 | 23.40 | 8.15 | 27.70 |
| **VTG‑R1** (RexTime) | **41.04** | **24.54** | **11.07** | **28.20** |
| VideoChat‑TPO | 34.53 | 19.26 | 9.81 | 25.23 |
| **VTG‑R1** (Charades) | **70.75** | **50.46** | **23.92** | **46.73** |
| VideoChat‑TPO | 58.3 | 40.2 | 18.4 | 38.1 |

*Key observations*  
- VTG‑R1 surpasses all baselines on every metric.  
- RL alone (without cold‑start) yields lower scores; high‑quality cold‑start data is essential.  
- Longer cold‑start response lengths (2048 tokens) further improve performance.  
- Filtering RL data (ε₂) is critical; unfiltered RL data degrades results.

## Limitations
1. **Data Dependence** – Requires high‑quality curated cold‑start data; hard to obtain in low‑resource domains.  
2. **Computational Overhead** – RL stage is resource‑intensive, potentially limiting adoption by smaller entities.  
3. **Future Work** – Needs exploration of data‑efficient RL, resource‑aware training, and broader applicability to other multimodal tasks.
