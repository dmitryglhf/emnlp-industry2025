# Summary

## TL;DR  
TeleBot is a fine‑tuned LLM that leverages a hybrid synthetic‑data pipeline—multi‑task extraction, role‑playing simulation, and session‑prefix completion—to generate high‑quality, strategy‑driven merchant telemarketing dialogues. In offline and online A/B tests, TeleBot outperforms a prompt‑engineering baseline and matches the top 25 % of human sales in high‑intention rate, demonstrating its industrial relevance for e‑commerce telemarketing.

## Problem & Motivation  
Merchant telemarketing requires a blend of task‑oriented and open‑domain dialogue, yet real‑world scripts are noisy (ASR errors, speaker diarization, hallucinations) and contain excessive chit‑chat, causing models to over‑learn casual turns and ignore expert strategies. Moreover, expert knowledge appears sparsely in scripts, limiting the model’s ability to proactively ask relevant questions. Existing fine‑tuning on raw scripts therefore fails to achieve strategy‑following and expert knowledge coverage needed for effective telemarketing.

## Approach  
1. **Unified Input Schema** – All data are expressed as `{System Prompt}{Profile}{Strategy}{Session}` where *Profile* is a merchant template, *Strategy* is a fine‑grained expert tip, and *Session* is the turn‑by‑turn dialogue.  
2. **Multi‑task Extraction** – A single LLM extracts *Profile*, *Strategy*, and *Session* from noisy scripts, cleaning vocabulary, merging turns, and adding brief turn‑level reasoning.  
3. **Role‑Playing Simulation** – Two off‑the‑shelf LLMs (e.g., GPT‑4) play salesperson and merchant roles, conditioned on the extracted *Profile* and *Strategy*, producing strategy‑following dialogues.  
4. **Session Prefix Completion** – Expert‑annotated Q&A pairs are used as the final turn; an LLM completes preceding turns conditioned on the same *Profile* and *Strategy*, injecting long‑tail expert knowledge.  
5. **Post‑Processing** – Multi‑layer filtering for conversationality, safety, duplication, and hallucination, yielding a 55 % retention rate.  
6. **Fine‑tuning** – TeleBot is trained on the union of the three synthetic sub‑datasets.

## Experimental Setup  
| Dataset | Seed | Valid | Avg. Turns | Avg. Utterance Length |
|---------|------|-------|------------|------------------------|
| Multi‑task Extraction | 1504 | 813 | 7.54 | 30.26 |
| Role‑Playing Simulation | 1710 | 1061 | 8.01 | 37.01 |
| Session Prefix Completion | 701 | 701 | 5.18 | 41.44 |

- **Offline Evaluation** – 300 sessions generated via Role‑Playing Simulation (salesperson role performed by TeleBot).  
  - Metrics: *Human‑likeness* (Consistency, Repetition, Conversationality), *Marketing Skills* (Diversity, Strategy‑following), *Hallucination* (Knowledge‑grounding).  
  - Evaluator: GPT‑4.1; Baselines: Prompt‑Engineering (PE), TeleBot, and two ablations (w/o extraction, w/o simulation).  
  - Human evaluation: 3 double‑blind annotators for PE and TeleBot.  

- **Online Evaluation** – A/B test with >20 k calls per system.  
  - Outcome metrics: *High Intention Rate* (calls >20 s that schedule offline meetings or provide contact info) and *Follow‑up Rate* (salesperson flags “good” or “potential customer”).

## Results  
| Metric | PE | TeleBot | w/o extraction | w/o simulation | Human PE | Human TeleBot |
|--------|----|---------|----------------|-----------------|----------|---------------|
| Consistency | 98.67 | 97.45 | 98.14 | 97.05 | 99.00 | 98.48 |
| Repetition | 75.47 | 93.20 | 91.50 | 92.07 | 99.09 | 99.13 |
| Conversationality | 90.14 | 94.44 | 90.98 | 96.03 | 91.55 | 97.84 |
| Diversity | 68.33 | 94.66 | 88.91 | 95.29 | 63.06 | 86.08 |
| Strategy‑following | 74.52 | 81.37 | 93.39 | 77.22 | 90.74 | 98.79 |
| Knowledge‑grounding | 69.81 | 76.69 | 85.71 | 61.38 | 85.66 | 90.15 |

*Best results are bolded; second best underlined.*  
TeleBot surpasses the PE baseline on all metrics, especially in Repetition, Diversity, and Strategy‑following. In online A/B tests, TeleBot’s high‑intention rate reaches the level of the top 25 % of human sales.
