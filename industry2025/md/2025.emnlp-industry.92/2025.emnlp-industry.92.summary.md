# Summary

## TL;DR  
HierDiffuse treats long‑term and short‑term user interests as a hierarchical denoising problem solved by a diffusion model. By introducing Semantic Guidance Disentanglement (SGD) to separate core interests from noisy short‑term signals and Trajectory Convergence Constraint (TCC) to collapse the reverse diffusion trajectory into a single mapping, the method achieves the best‑ever AUC on Amazon, Taobao, and an industrial e‑commerce dataset while keeping inference throughput high enough for online CTR prediction.

## Problem & Motivation  
CTR prediction in recommendation and advertising systems must reconcile a user’s enduring preferences (captured by long‑term behavior) with momentary contextual cues (captured by short‑term interactions). Existing fusion strategies—concatenation, cross‑scale attention, gating—treat the two scales as independent and fuse them only at the end, leading to representational misalignment and a lack of noise robustness. In real‑world deployments, accidental clicks or exploratory browsing can corrupt short‑term signals, and the mismatch in feature granularity between long‑ and short‑term encoders further degrades performance. The paper therefore targets a principled fusion framework that jointly optimizes multi‑scale interest representation and adapts to noisy transient signals.

## Approach  
HierDiffuse redefines interest fusion as a conditional denoising process:

1. **Cross‑scale diffusion** – the long‑term latent vector **h**⁽ᴸ⁾ is progressively noised over *T* steps, while the short‑term representation **h**⁽ˢ⁾ (obtained by average‑pooling the session encodings) conditions the reverse denoising network **µθ**.  
2. **Semantic Guidance Disentanglement (SGD)** – extends conventional CFG by decomposing the guidance into multiple semantic components *sᵢ(xₜ, yᵢ)*, each weighted by an adaptive factor *ωᵢ*. This allows the model to treat distinct short‑term behavior categories (e.g., niche clicks vs trend‑driven clicks) differently during denoising.  
3. **Trajectory Convergence Constraint (TCC)** – learns a deterministic mapping **Φ(xₜ, t)** that directly predicts the clean latent **x₀** from any noisy state **xₜ**, enforcing that all intermediate steps map to the same target. This collapses the 100–1000 neural‑function evaluations typical of DDPM/DDIM into a single forward pass, dramatically reducing inference latency while preserving generation quality.

The overall pipeline (Fig. 1) therefore consists of a transformer‑based long‑term encoder, a transformer‑based short‑term encoder, the diffusion denoising module with SGD, and the TCC‑accelerated reverse trajectory.

## Experimental Setup  
| Item | Details |
|------|----------|
| **Datasets** | Amazon Book (McAuley et al., 2015), Taobao (Aliyun Tianchi dataset), and a proprietary industrial e‑commerce dataset (14‑day training, 15th‑day evaluation). |
| **Dataset sizes** | Amazon: 75,053 users, 358,367 items, 150,016 interactions. <br>Taobao: 7,956,431 users, 34,196,612 items, 7,956,431 interactions. |
| **Baselines** | Avg‑Pooling DNN, DIN, DIEN, DiffuRec, DreamRec. |
| **Evaluation metric** | AUC (area under the ROC curve). |
| **Inference throughput** | Measured in batches inferred per second. |
| **Training** | A100 GPUs, cosine noise schedule (Eq. 10–11), curriculum for TCC (Δt grows from ≤0.1 T to U(0.1 T, 0.5 T)), λ decays linearly from 1.0 to 0.2. |
| **Implementation** | Reverse backbone: MLP (default); alternative transformer backbone tested. |

## Results  
| Model | Amazon | Taobao | Industrial |
|-------|--------|--------|------------|
| Avg‑Pooling DNN | 0.7689 | 0.8539 | 0.7512 |
| DIN | 0.7862 | 0.8995 | 0.7564 |
| DIEN | 0.8377 | 0.9222 | 0.7611 |
| DiffuRec | 0.8395 | 0.9258 | 0.7607 |
| DreamRec | 0.8421 | 0.9286 | 0.7619 |
| **HierDiffuse** | **0.8472** | **0.9327** | **0.7680** |

*Relative gains*: 0.0083 over DreamRec on Amazon, 0.0041 over DreamRec on Taobao, 0.0061 over DreamRec on Industrial.

**Ablation highlights** (Industrial dataset):

| Component | AUC | Δ vs full | Inference (batches/s) |
|-----------|-----|-----------|------------------------|
| SGD (ours) | 0.7680 | – | 16.1 |
| CFG (Ho & Salimans) | 0.7663 | –0.0017 | 16.9 |
| TCC (ours) | 0.7680 | – | 16.1 |
| DDPM | 0.7682 | +0.0002 | 9.98 |
| DDIM | 0.7681 | +0.0001 | 13.01 |
| Transformer reverse backbone | 0.7684 | +0.0004 | 14.8 |
| MLP reverse backbone | 0.7680 | – | 16.1 |

The ablation confirms that each module (SGD, TCC, MLP backbone, diffusion direction Long→Short) contributes to the final performance and that the inference speed remains high enough for production (≈16 batches/s).
