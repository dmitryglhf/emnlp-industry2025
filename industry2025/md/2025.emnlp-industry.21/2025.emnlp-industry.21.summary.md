# Summary

## TL;DR  
The paper introduces **APARL**, a dual‑loop reinforcement‑learning framework that combines an adaptive perplexity‑aware sampling strategy with rule‑guided RL to train large language models for abnormal event detection in customer‑service dialogues. APARL achieves a **17.19 % average F1 boost** over all baselines and a **9.59 % improvement** on out‑of‑domain (OOD) tests, outperforming leading API models such as DeepSeek‑R1. The method is positioned as a ready‑to‑deploy solution for industrial platforms like Meituan’s food‑delivery service.

## Problem & Motivation  
Real‑world customer‑service conversations contain complex, dynamic interactions that make abnormal event detection difficult. Existing small BERT‑based classifiers require costly annotation (≈120 h per event) and do not scale to new event types. Large‑language‑model (LLM) few‑shot prompting is prompt‑sensitive and fails in vertical domains, while supervised fine‑tuned LLMs overfit and lack OOD generalization. The paper targets a robust, scalable solution that can adapt quickly to new scenarios and deliver high commercial value.

## Approach  
**APARL** is a *dualloop* architecture:

| Loop | Purpose | Key Design |
|------|---------|------------|
| **Outer** | Dynamically adjust sample difficulty based on model proficiency | *Perplexity‑aware sampling* (Eq. 2) that peaks at the current average proficiency μₚ, shifting curriculum from easy to hard examples. |
| **Inner** | Optimize reasoning policy with domain knowledge | *Rule‑guided RL* using a modified DAPO algorithm (Eq. 3) with a KL‑divergence regularizer (Eq. 5). Reward (Eq. 6) penalizes formatting errors and rewards correct answers. |

The RL state is \(S=\{s_t=(e_p,u_{t-k:t})\}\), actions are the 20 predefined abnormal event types \(A=\{a_1,\dots,a_M\}\), and the reward combines accuracy and format penalties.

## Experimental Setup  
- **Dataset**: 55 k training and 9 k test dialogues from Meituan’s online logs, multi‑turn, 20 abnormal event types.  
- **Baselines**:  
  - Small model: **BGE‑M3**  
  - API calls: **GPT‑4o**, **o1‑preview**, **DeepSeek‑V3**, **DeepSeek‑R1**  
  - Supervised fine‑tuned (SFT) on base models  
  - RL methods: **GRPO**, **DAPO** vs. **APARL**  
- **Base Models**: **Qwen‑14B‑Instruct** and **DeepSeek‑R1‑Distill‑Qwen‑14B**.  
- **Metrics**: Precision, Recall, F1 on the 9 k test set.

- **APARL** outperforms all baselines, including the best API model (DeepSeek‑R1, 74.37 % F1).  
- **Average improvement**: **17.19 %** over all baselines; **9.59 %** on OOD tests (reported in the abstract).  
- **Ablation** (Table 2) shows APARL surpasses GRPO and DAPO by 4.89 % and 7.93 % in F1, respectively.

## Industry Impact  
The paper claims that APARL is “a superior solution for the industrial application of abnormal detection models, contributing to improved operational efficiency and economic benefits.” It is deployed within Meituan’s food‑delivery platform, where abnormal event detection directly influences customer satisfaction and platform retention.

## Limitations  
The authors do not explicitly list limitations; no caveats are reported in the paper.
