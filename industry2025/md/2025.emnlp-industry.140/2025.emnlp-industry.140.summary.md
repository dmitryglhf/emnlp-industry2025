# Summary

## TL;DR  
Airbnb’s production‑grade incremental summarization system uses a fine‑tuned Mixtral‑8×7B LLM to generate concise bullet notes in real time, filtered by a DeBERTa‑based relevance classifier.  Agent edits are captured online and used to retrain the LLM offline, closing a feedback loop that yields a 3 % reduction in case handling time (up to 9 % for highly complex cases) and high agent satisfaction (> 80 % positive).

---

## Problem & Motivation  
Customer‑support agents spend roughly 10 % of their case‑handling time writing mandatory notes, which forces frequent context switches and slows resolution.  Existing post‑conversation summarizers cannot provide real‑time, incremental support, and they lack precise update mechanisms for long, multi‑source interactions.  The paper tackles the need for an on‑the‑fly summarizer that reduces agent effort while maintaining high factual accuracy.

---

## Approach  
1. **Progressive Note‑Taking** – The system generates new bullets only when the LLM detects substantial new information.  
2. **LLM‑based Continuous Bullet Generation** – A Mixtral‑8×7B model is fine‑tuned on internal data and invoked with a *prefix‑prompt* that includes all previously accepted bullets, ensuring edits persist across turns.  
3. **Bullet Relevance Classifier** – A DeBERTa model filters out trivial or redundant bullets.  It keeps only five target classes (customer‑provides‑issue, customer‑provides‑context, customer‑takes‑action, agent‑asks‑follow‑up, agent‑provides‑solution).  In production it operates as a binary filter with an F1 of 0.895.  
4. **Agent‑Edits Learning Framework** – Agent edits are logged as “before‑edit” and “after‑edit” pairs.  These pairs are verified by an LLM judge and human review; only high‑quality improvements are used to fine‑tune the LLM via supervised fine‑tuning (SFT) and preference alignment (DPO/ORPO).

---

## Experimental Setup  
| Item | Details |
|------|----------|
| **Datasets** | 1,200 random sampled production case conversations (offline).  Public benchmarks: SAMSum (Gliwa et al., 2019) and DialSum (Chen et al., 2021). |
| **Baselines** | Mixtral‑Base (un‑fine‑tuned), Mixtral‑NF (task‑specific fine‑tuning without agent edits), Mixtral‑FB (with agent‑edit feedback), GPT‑4o (external benchmark). |
| **Evaluation Metrics** | Offline: completeness, truthfulness, conciseness, overall (average of the three).  Online: agent satisfaction (post‑pilot survey), agent working time, customer NPS. |
| **Serving Configurations** | LLM inference via NVIDIA Triton/TensorRT‑LLM on 2 A100 GPUs (p50 = 600 ms, p95 = 2 s).  Bullet classifier on A10G GPU (p50 = 20 ms, p95 = 40 ms). |

## Results  

### Offline  
| Model | Completeness | Truthfulness | Conciseness | Overall |
|-------|--------------|--------------|-------------|---------|
| Mixtral‑Base | 0.585 ± 0.0138 | 0.989 ± 0.0035 | 0.788 ± 0.0109 | 0.787 ± 0.0053 |
| Mixtral‑NF | 0.824 ± 0.0107 | 0.995 ± 0.0012 | 0.819 ± 0.0093 | 0.879 ± 0.0047 |
| Mixtral‑FB | 0.842 ± 0.0011 | 0.996 ± 0.0019 | 0.818 ± 0.0095 | 0.885 ± 0.0049 |
| GPT‑4o | 0.846 ± 0.0073 | 0.995 ± 0.0008 | 0.788 ± 0.0134 | 0.876 ± 0.0053 |

*Significance*: Mixtral‑FB vs NF (p = 4.6 × 10⁻⁴), Mixtral‑FB vs GPT‑4o (p = 4.1 × 10⁻⁵), Mixtral‑NF vs GPT‑4o (p = 0.202).

### Online (Diff‑in‑Diff)  
*Agent Satisfaction* – 95.2 % (English), 81.8 % (French), 89.4 % (Spanish) reported “Satisfied” or “Very Satisfied”.  
*Case Handling Time* – 3 % reduction overall, up to 9 % for highly complex cases compared to bulk summarization.
