# Summary

## TL;DR  
The paper introduces **Region Comprehension Index (RCI)**, a model‑based score that quantifies whether a multimodal benchmark relies on global image understanding or can be solved with localized visual cues. By comparing a reference model’s performance on full images versus the best‑performing patch, RCI reveals spatial reasoning biases across 13 widely used vision‑language datasets, guiding industry practitioners to curate benchmarks that better reflect real‑world deployment needs.

## Problem & Motivation  
Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) excel on many vision‑language benchmarks, yet it is unclear if these benchmarks truly test global reasoning or allow success via narrow, localized visual shortcuts. Existing evaluation metrics (e.g., CIDEr, BLEU, CLIPScore) focus on linguistic alignment or diversity and do not expose whether a task requires holistic image understanding. In industrial settings—autonomous driving, medical imaging, document intelligence, content moderation—models must reliably integrate information across an entire scene; failure to diagnose local‑vs‑global reasoning can lead to unreliable deployed systems.

## Approach  
RCI is defined as  
\[
RCI_n = 1 - \frac{MPP_n}{FIP},
\]
where **FIP** is the aggregated model performance on the full image and **MPP\(_n\)** is the aggregated performance on the best‑performing patch at granularity \(n\).  
- **Patch‑based evaluation**: each image is split into an \(n\times n\) grid (with \(n=1,2,3\)).  
- **Reference models**: InternVL‑2.5‑1B, Qwen2‑VL‑2B, and Molmo‑1B.  
- **Interpretation bands**: RCI ≥ +0.30 → strong global; RCI ≤ −0.30 → strong local; intermediate values indicate balanced or moderate reasoning.  
- **Spatial bias analysis**: for each patch position, the fraction of total correct predictions contributed by that patch is computed, revealing whether certain regions (e.g., the image center) dominate performance.

## Experimental Setup  
- **Datasets**: 13 vision‑language benchmarks – AI2D, BLINK, MMStar, ScienceQA, RealWorldQA, AMBER, MME, POPE, HallusionBench, GQA, ChartQA, TextVQA, VizWiz.  
- **Patch granularities**: \(n \in \{2,3\}\) (coarse and fine).  
- **Reference models**: InternVL‑2.5‑1B, Qwen2‑VL‑2B, Molmo‑1B.  
- **Evaluation metrics**: RCI, FIP, MPP.  
- **Protocol**: offline evaluation; smaller models (1 B) are shown to correlate highly with larger variants (r ≥ 0.9), enabling efficient audits.  
- **Human study**: annotators performed tasks using individual patches versus full images to validate RCI trends.

- **Cross‑model correlation**: RCI values are highly correlated across architectures (r ≥ 0.91), confirming that the global‑vs‑local bias is intrinsic to the datasets, not a model artifact.  
- **Human alignment**: tasks with high RCI were harder for humans when restricted to patches, mirroring model trends.  
- **Spatial bias**: central patch (Patch 5) consistently contributes the most to MPP, indicating a pervasive center bias across benchmarks.  
- **Benchmark insights**:  
  - *Local‑reasoning‑heavy*: BLINK, HallusionBench, MMStar, RealWorldQA, GQA, AI2D.  
  - *Global‑reasoning‑heavy*: ChartQA, ScienceQA, TextVQA.
