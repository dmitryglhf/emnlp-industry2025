# Summary

## TL;DR  
MVP‑RAG introduces a two‑stage retrieval‑generation pipeline for Product Attribute Value Identification (PAVI). By first retrieving candidate attribute values with TACLR and similar products with BGE‑base, then generating standardized values with a fine‑tuned Qwen2.5‑7B‑Instruct, the method achieves a new state‑of‑the‑art F1 of **89.5 %** on the proprietary Xianyu‑PAVI dataset and **79.4 %** on WDC‑PAVI, outperforming all baselines by 3–26 % and has been deployed in a real‑world e‑commerce platform.

## Problem & Motivation  
Accurate attribute values are critical for search, recommendation, and analytics on e‑commerce sites. Existing PAVI methods suffer from cascading errors (two‑stage), inability to handle out‑of‑distribution (OOD) values (classification), hallucinations (generation), and threshold sensitivity (retrieval). The paper targets these gaps by redefining PAVI as a retrieval‑generation task that leverages both product and attribute‑value contexts.

## Approach  
**Multi‑level Retrieval**  
1. *Attribute‑value retrieval*: Encode product title+description with TACLR; encode attribute values with prompts “*a category with attribute being value*”; retrieve top‑k candidates per category.  
2. *Product retrieval*: Encode products with BGE‑base; compute cosine similarity; restrict to the same category to obtain few‑shot product examples.  

**Attribute‑value Generation**  
Combine the retrieved products and candidate values into a five‑part prompt (task definition, note, similar products, product info, candidate values). Fine‑tune Qwen2.5‑7B‑Instruct on this prompt; training objective is next‑word prediction with the prompt prefix excluded from loss.  

Key design choices: use of TACLR for retrieval (state‑of‑the‑art), BGE‑base for product embeddings, and Qwen2.5 for generation to control hallucinations while handling OOD values.

## Experimental Setup  
| Dataset | Train | Valid | Test | PA Pairs | Null Pairs |
|---------|-------|-------|-------|----------|------------|
| Xianyu‑PAVI | 809,528 | 81,699 | 85,024 | 3,584,462 | 2,345,577 |
| WDC‑PAVI | 1,066 | – | 354 | 8,832 | 3,973 (train) / 1,330 (test) |

**Metrics**: micro‑averaged precision@1, recall@1, F1@1; Coverage for non‑empty ground truth.  

**Baselines**:  
- BERT‑CLS (classification)  
- LLM (generation)  
- Product‑RAG (generation + product retrieval)  
- TACLR (retrieval)  

**Implementation**:  
- Retrieval: BGE‑base, cosine similarity, same‑category restriction; TACLR for attribute values.  
- Generation: Qwen2.5‑7B‑Instruct, 3 epochs, batch 16, AdamW, lr 2e‑5, 1 % warmup, cosine scheduler.

## Results  
| Method | Xianyu‑PAVI Precision | Recall | F1 | WDC‑PAVI Precision | Recall | F1 |
|--------|-----------------------|--------|-----|---------------------|--------|-----|
| BERT‑CLS | 50.9 | 50.1 | 50.5 | 68.9 | 12.0 | 20.5 |
| Qwen2.5 (zero‑shot) | 42.7 | 55.7 | 48.4 | 51.9 | 60.3 | 55.8 |
| Qwen2.5 (few‑shot) | 45.8 | 58.6 | 51.4 | 72.2 | 72.3 | 72.2 |
| Qwen2.5 (Product‑RAG) | 58.3 | 69.1 | 63.2 | 75.1 | 73.4 | 74.2 |
| Qwen2.5 (fine‑tune) | 84.5 | 79.1 | 81.7 | 54.1 | 60.0 | 56.9 |
| TACLR | 85.4 | 87.1 | 86.2 | 74.3 | 70.9 | 72.6 |
| **MVP‑RAG** | **93.8** | **85.3** | **89.5** | **81.1** | **77.7** | **79.4** |

*Key gains*: MVP‑RAG outperforms Product‑RAG by **26.3 %** on F1 and TACLR by **3.3 %** on Xianyu‑PAVI.  

**Ablation** (Xianyu‑PAVI / WDC‑PAVI)  
| Method | Precision | Recall | F1 |
|--------|------------|--------|-----|
| Qwen2.5 (fine‑tune) | 84.5 | 79.1 | 81.7 |
| TACLR | 85.4 | 87.1 | 86.2 |
| w/o product_retrieve | 93.2 | 85.5 | 89.2 |
| w/o value_retrieve | 91.5 | 83.0 | 87.0 |
| **MVP‑RAG** | **93.8** | **85.3** | **89.5** |

*Impact of product counts*: Coverage rises from 56.3 % to 78.5 % as retrieved product number increases; F1 remains stable.  
*Impact of attribute‑value counts*: Coverage climbs from 94.9 % to 99.6 %; recall peaks at 89.2 % when candidate values = 6; precision declines with more candidates.
