# Summary

## TL;DR
The paper introduces the Similar Question Generation (SQG) task to augment retrieval‑based customer‑service chatbots with semantically consistent yet syntactically diverse “similar questions.”  By fine‑tuning ChatGLM2 with a one‑to‑many, intention‑enhanced objective and a budget‑constrained greedy selection of in‑context demonstrations, the authors achieve a 92 % user‑satisfaction rate—an 18 % lift over an unaugmented baseline—while improving qualitative diversity by 4.74 % and relative performance by 120 % on a real‑world Chinese financial‑sector dataset.

## Problem & Motivation
Retrieval‑based chatbots rely on a static, human‑verified QA base to guarantee compliance and avoid hallucinations.  In practice, customer queries are highly diverse; a single failure in query matching can break the interaction.  Existing data‑augmentation methods (SimBERT, RoFORMER‑Sim) either lack contextual awareness or produce repetitive outputs.  The paper tackles the need for a cost‑effective, offline knowledge‑base expansion that preserves semantic consistency while increasing expression diversity, a requirement that is critical for regulated domains such as finance.

## Approach
1. **SQG Task Definition** – Generate a set of *similar questions* that retain the intent of a source question while varying phrasing.  
2. **One‑to‑Many Generation** – The LLM (ChatGLM2) is prompted to output multiple questions in a single pass, conditioning each new question on previously generated ones to reduce repetition.  
3. **Intention‑Enhanced Batch Generation** – The source answer is added to the prompt, expanding the semantic exploration space (Figure 2).  
4. **Refined Training Objective** –  
   \[
   \mathcal{L}_{Intention}=\sum_{i}\sum_{j}\sum_{l=1}^{L}\mathcal{L}_{j+l}(q_i,a)
   \]  
   where each \(\mathcal{L}_{j+l}\) conditions on the QA pair and all prior generated questions.  
5. **Dynamic Demonstration Selection** – For a target question \(q_s\), a greedy algorithm (Algorithm 2) selects \(K\) demonstrations maximizing relevance \(S(q_s,q_{p_i})\) and pairwise diversity \(\operatorname{dist}(q_{p_i},q_{p_j})\) with \(\alpha=0.5\).  
6. **Budget‑Constrained Similar Question Selection** – Formulated as  
   \[
   \max_{S\subseteq Q}\sum_{\substack{q,q\in S\\q\neq q}}\operatorname{dist}(q_a,q_b)\quad\text{s.t.}\quad\sum_{q\in S}\operatorname{cost}(q)\le B
   \]  
   and solved by a greedy algorithm (Algorithm 1) with a \(1-1/e\) approximation bound.

## Experimental Setup
- **Dataset**: 3,000+ Chinese QA pairs from an active WeBank customer‑service chatbot; each pair has an average of 40 human‑annotated similar questions.  
- **Training**: 90,000 instances sampled from the raw QA pairs.  
- **Evaluation**:  
  - 90 unseen QA pairs, each with ~45 reference questions.  
  - 15 new questions from recent chatbot logs for human assessment.  
- **Metrics**:  
  - *Semantic Relevance*: Precision, Recall, F1 (BERTScore).  
  - *Lexical Diversity*: Distinct‑1, Distinct‑2, Distinct‑Avg.  
  - *Human Acceptance Ratio*: % of generated questions deemed acceptable by five industry experts.  
- **Baselines & Models**:  
  - SimBERT, RoFORMER‑Sim, ChatGLM2 (Zero‑Shot), ChatGLM2 (Few‑Shot), ChatGLM2‑FT, Context‑Aware (Ours), Intention‑Enhanced (Ours), + dynamic demo selection.

- **Human Evaluation**:  
  - SimBERT & RoFORMER‑Sim: only 20 % of generated questions met the acceptance criteria.  
  - ChatGLM2‑FT: 37.9 % acceptance.  
  - Context‑Aware: modest impact on acceptance ratio.  
  - Intention‑Enhanced: 84 % acceptance, the highest among all methods.  
- **Deployment**: The augmented chatbot achieved a 92 % user‑satisfaction rate, an 18 % improvement over the unaugmented baseline.

## Industry Impact
The system was deployed within WeBank’s customer‑service chatbot, operating in a regulated financial environment.  The augmentation pipeline runs offline, adding ~20 similar questions per source QA, and incurs negligible runtime latency.  The 18 % lift in user satisfaction translated into measurable business gains: reduced call‑center load, faster response times, and higher compliance adherence.
