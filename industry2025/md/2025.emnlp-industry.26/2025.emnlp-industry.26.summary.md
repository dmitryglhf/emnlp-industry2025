# Summary

## TL;DR  
MADS (Multi‑Agent Dialogue Simulation) is a closed‑loop framework that generates high‑quality persuasive dialogues by orchestrating three agents—User, Dialog, and Optimization—using personality signifiers such as Zodiac signs and MBTI types.  The system is validated through a Chain‑of‑Attitude (CoA) model and LLM‑based persuasion benchmarks, and when applied to a real‑world marketing scenario it boosted the organic traffic conversion rate by **22.4 %** (from **1.83 %** to **2.24 %**).

## Problem & Motivation  
Persuasive dialogue systems suffer from three practical bottlenecks:  
1. **Lack of authentic user data** – existing corpora rarely contain multi‑turn dialogues with rich user profiles.  
2. **Cold‑start evaluation difficulty** – new systems lack interaction logs, making benchmark scores unreliable.  
3. **Low prompt‑engineering efficiency** – prompts are usually hand‑crafted and brittle across roles and contexts.  
MADS addresses these gaps by self‑playing agents that simulate diverse personas and evaluate outcomes automatically, enabling scalable data generation for cold‑start deployments.

## Approach  
MADS follows a **Meta‑Instruction → Simulation → Optimization → Domain‑Specific LLM** pipeline.  
- **User Agents**: generate structured user profiles with tags (demographics, Zodiac signs, MBTI types, business attributes).  
- **Dialog Agent**: engages in multi‑turn persuasion, initially using a concise prompt and iteratively refining it via a reflection mechanism (Algorithm 1).  
- **Optimization Agent**: contains three sub‑modules:  
  - **Summary**: classifies each turn’s attitude with an LLM‑based classifier \(L^{attitude}\).  
  - **Evaluation**: computes a first‑order Markov transition matrix \(T_{ij}\), entropy \(H_{avg}\), and Jensen‑Shannon divergence \(JS\) against a baseline.  
  - **Evolution**: updates the Dialog Agent prompt when the acceptance rate \(\theta\) and entropy exceed thresholds.  
The CoA framework defines **16 attitude states** and models their transitions, providing a quantitative measure of attitude diversity.

## Experimental Setup  
| Component | Details |
|-----------|----------|
| **Datasets** | DailyPersuasion (DPx subsets), synthetic insurance scenario, MMP (Make Me Pay), P4G (Persuasion For Good) |
| **Baselines** | Dbase (demographics only), Dsign (adds Zodiac), Dmbti (adds MBTI), Dbusi (adds business attributes) |
| **Models** | GPT‑4o, Mistral‑7B, ERNIE‑Lite, Qwen2.5‑7B (fine‑tuned on MADS data) |
| **Evaluation Metrics** | Havg, Hnorm, JS (attitude diversity); Cstr, σ, CV (strategy diversity); Donation % / Withdraw % (MMP); conversion rate (marketing scenario) |
| **Deployment Context** | Offline fine‑tuning of small LLMs; offline evaluation with Claude‑3.5 and MMP; real‑world marketing deployment measuring organic traffic conversion |
