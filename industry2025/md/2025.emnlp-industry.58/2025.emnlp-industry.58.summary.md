# Summary

## TL;DR  
The paper introduces two new clinical datasets—**SYNUR** (synthetic nurse dictations) and **SIMORD** (simulated medical orders)—and evaluates closed‑weight large language models (LLMs) on the tasks of extracting structured observations from nurse dictations and orders from doctor‑patient conversations.  GPT‑4o, GPT‑4.1, and the 3.8 B Mediphi‑Instruct model achieve the best performance, demonstrating that lightweight open‑weight LLMs can match or exceed the flagship GPT‑4o on these real‑world tasks.

## Problem & Motivation  
Clinical documentation is time‑consuming; nurses and physicians often rely on dictations that must be translated into structured EHR flowsheets or order sets.  Existing NLP work focuses on benchmark datasets and does not address the long, disfluent, and highly structured nature of nurse dictations or the free‑form fields in order extraction.  The paper argues that high‑quality, real‑world solutions are needed to reduce provider burden and improve patient care.

## Approach  
The authors build two pipelines:

1. **Nurse Observation Extraction** – a three‑step process (Segmentation → Retrieval‑Augmented Generation (RAG) → Extraction) that ingests a nurse’s spoken assessment, filters the flowsheet schema to the most relevant rows, and uses an LLM to extract and canonicalise observations.  
2. **Medical Order Extraction** – a prompt‑based system that outputs orders in JSON with four attributes (Description, Reason, Type, Provenance).  The same prompt is used for zero‑shot and few‑shot settings.

Both pipelines rely on closed‑weight LLMs (GPT‑4o, GPT‑4o‑mini, GPT‑4.1, GPT‑4.1‑mini) and, for order extraction, also on open‑weight models (Phi3.5‑mini‑instruct, Mediphi‑Instruct, Llama3‑8B‑instruct, Llama3‑Med42‑8B).

## Experimental Setup  
- **Datasets**  
  - *SYNUR*: 223 synthetic dictations, 3 000 observations, 185 tokens on average.  
  - *Hospital datasets*: 3 partner hospitals (H1, H2, H3) with 49, 50, 50 transcripts each (149 total) and 1 788 observations.  
  - *SIMORD*: 100 test examples (255 orders) from ACI‑Bench (207 conversations) and PriMock57 (57 conversations).  
- **Baselines** – closed‑weight LLMs: GPT‑4o, GPT‑4o‑mini, GPT‑4.1, GPT‑4.1‑mini; open‑weight LLMs: Phi3.5‑mini‑instruct, Mediphi‑Instruct, Llama3‑8B‑instruct, Llama3‑Med42‑8B.  
- **Metrics** – for nurse extraction: f1.  For order extraction: Match, Description, Reason, Type, Provenance (all reported as percentages).  
- **Prompting** – zero‑shot and few‑shot (64 training samples) with identical prompts (see Appendix A.1).  
- **Evaluation** – LLM‑as‑a‑Judge (GPT‑5) for quality/realism of SYNUR; inter‑annotator agreement (Cohen’s κ = 0.634) on a 10 % sample of SYNUR.
