# Summary

## TL;DR  
The paper introduces a bidirectional reasoning‑based fine‑tuning strategy for multilingual financial sustainability classification. By supervising large language models (LLMs) with both positive and negative reasons alongside labels, the method consistently outperforms label‑only and unidirectional‑reason baselines across English, Hindi, Bengali, and Telugu, enabling smaller models to match or exceed larger ones—an advantage for industry deployments.

## Problem & Motivation  
Financial decision‑making requires accurate, timely analysis of domain‑specific documents in multiple languages. Existing research largely focuses on high‑resource languages, leaving low‑resource languages (e.g., Bengali, Telugu) underserved. This gap hampers stakeholders in emerging markets, leading to delayed or inaccurate risk assessments. The paper seeks to bridge this gap by improving multilingual financial sustainability classification.

## Approach  
- **Core Idea**: Fine‑tune LLMs with a *bidirectional reasoning* objective: predict the label **and** generate a *positive* reason (why the statement is sustainable/unsustainable) and a *negative* reason (why it is not the opposite).  
- **Pipeline**:  
  1. **Data**: Financial statements \(S\) with labels \(Y\) and automatically generated reasons \(R^+, R^-\).  
  2. **Model**: Pre‑trained LLM (e.g., LLaMA‑3.2 3B, Qwen‑2.5 1.5B).  
  3. **Loss**: Cross‑entropy over the tuple \((\hat{y}, \hat{r}^+, \hat{r}^-)\) against ground truth.  
- **Design Choices**:  
  - Reasons generated by GPT‑40 to avoid manual annotation.  
  - Reason length capped at 150 tokens.  
  - Parameter‑efficient fine‑tuning (PEFT) used to keep training efficient.  

## Experimental Setup  
- **Languages**: English, Hindi, Bengali, Telugu.  
- **Baselines**:  
  - Label‑only fine‑tuning (FT(L)).  
  - Label + unidirectional reasoning (FT(L,R)).  
  - Label + bidirectional reasoning (FT(L,R⁺,R⁻)) – the proposed method.  
- **Evaluation Metrics**: Accuracy, Precision, Recall, F1.  
- **Models**:  
  - LLaMA‑3.2 (3B) – “♦” (label‑only), “♠” (bidirectional).  
  - Qwen‑2.5 (1.5B) – “♦” (label‑only), “♠” (bidirectional).  
- **Deployment Context**: Not reported.

**Key Takeaways**  
- Bidirectional reasoning improves accuracy by ~2 % over label‑only across all languages.  
- Smaller models (Qwen‑2.5 1.5B) with bidirectional reasoning approach the performance of larger LLaMA‑3.2 3B models.  
- No reasoning is applied during evaluation; the improvement comes solely from the training objective.
