# Summary

## TL;DR  
ISEE is an instruction‑tuned, SOP‑enhanced quality evaluator for e‑commerce product pages. By automatically surfacing customer‑impacting features from search logs, vendor data, and feedback, and by training a 12B Pixtral‑12B LLM to reliably follow structured SOPs, ISEE achieves **90 % precision at 78 % recall** across 20 product categories and 150 features—outperforming larger (>200 B) models while cutting operational cost by ~80 %.

## Problem & Motivation  
High‑quality product content is essential for customer trust and conversions. Existing defect‑detection workflows treat all features equally and rely on manually crafted prompts, leading to wasted effort on low‑impact issues and high prompt‑engineering cost. In e‑commerce, incomplete or inaccurate information (e.g., conflicting RAM values, missing size specs) directly hurts conversion rates.

## Approach  
1. **Customer‑aware feature prioritization** – ISEE synthesizes four signals (search intent, vendor emphasis, post‑sale feedback via InsightNet, and LLM‑generated bootstrapping) to rank vertical features.  
2. **Instruction‑aligned defect detection** – A 12B Pixtral‑12B LLM is instruction‑tuned with a templated prompt that includes a base prompt (B), an optional structured SOP block (S), and a gold output (O). Structured SOPs contain fields such as `evaluation_scope`, `ignore`, `valid_format`, and `constraints`.  
3. **Zero‑shot generalization** – The instruction‑tuning exposes the model to synthetic SOPs and real SOPs, enabling it to handle unseen features and categories.  
4. **Multi‑Agent adjudication** – Three diverse LLMs (Claude‑3.7 Sonnet, DeepSeek‑R1‑Distill‑Qwen‑32B, Pixtral‑12B) vote on each defect candidate; majority consensus yields final labels, with ambiguous cases flagged as “subjective”.  
5. **Production & self‑service workflows** – In production, a guardrail‑LLM routes outputs into “defective”, “non‑defective”, and “subjective” paths, with a Multi‑Agent Adjudicator validating classifications. A self‑service mode allows domain experts to auto‑prompt and fine‑tune until precision‑recall thresholds are met.

## Experimental Setup  
- **Dataset**: 20 diverse categories (electronics, apparel, home, etc.), 1000 products per category, top‑15 features from the prioritized set, 180 k synthetic examples (conflicts, inaccuracies, omissions).  
- **Baselines**: Claude‑3.7 Sonnet, Pixtral‑12B.  
- **Evaluation metrics**: Precision, Recall, F1; cost per 10 M products.  
- **Training**: Instruction‑tune Pixtral‑12B with LoRA, 8‑bit quantization, learning rate 2e‑4, batch size 32, sequence length 2048, 8× A100 GPUs for 24 h.  
- **Deployment context**: Production workflow (continuous defect evaluation, feedback loop) and self‑service workflow (auto‑prompting).  

## Results  
| Model | Precision | Recall | F1 |
|-------|-----------|--------|-----|
| **ISEE** (base) | 0.87 | 0.78 | 0.83 |
| Claude‑3.7 Sonnet | 0.75 | 0.70 | 0.72 |
| Pixtral‑12B | 0.72 | 0.65 | 0.68 |

- **SOP‑Injected Performance**: ISEE achieves **F1 = 0.84** at a fixed cost of **$40 K per 10 M products** (Table 1). Claude incurs a higher cost of **$270 K** (Figure 3).  
- **Task‑specific SOP F1** (Table 3): ISEE outperforms all models on conflicts (0.87 vs. 0.85), inaccuracies (0.89 vs. 0.83), and omissions (0.85 vs. 0.86).  
- **Auto‑prompting**: ISEE improves from 0.83 to **0.87** after iterative refinement; Claude rises to 0.82.  
- **Zero‑shot generalization**: 92 % category coverage, zero‑shot F1 = 0.78.
