# Summary

## TL;DR
Dr.Copilot is a multi‑agent large‑language‑model system that gives Romanian doctors real‑time, interpretable feedback on the *presentation* of their written responses in a telemedicine platform. By optimizing prompts with DSPy on only 100 annotated examples, the system achieves measurable gains: a 10 % improvement in quality metrics and an 83.89 % rise in positive patient reviews, demonstrating one of the first real‑world deployments of LLMs in Romanian medical settings.

## Problem & Motivation
Telemedicine has surged since 2020, yet doctors often prioritize medical accuracy over how they communicate advice. Existing onboarding guidelines are too general and are frequently ignored, leading to negative patient reviews that are driven mainly by presentation quality (e.g., empathy, grammatical correctness, relevance). In Romanian, a low‑resource language, there is a lack of medical NLP tools and open‑weight models suitable for deployment. Dr.Copilot addresses this gap by providing doctors with actionable, case‑by‑case feedback on 17 interpretable quality measures, aiming to improve patient satisfaction and, consequently, platform retention and business outcomes.

## Approach
Dr.Copilot is built around three LLM agents:

1. **Scorer Agent** – evaluates a doctor’s response on 17 quality metrics (empathy, problems addressed, grammatical correctness, specificity, relevance, informativeness, platform‑functionality usage, etc.).  
2. **Recommender Agent** – generates tailored, actionable suggestions to improve the response presentation, based on the scores from the Scorer.  
3. **Reconciliation Agent** – self‑evaluates the effect of the recommendations by revising the original response and re‑scoring it.

All prompts are automatically optimized with **DSPy** (using Labeled Few‑Shot, Bootstrap Few‑Shot, and SIMBA optimizers). The system relies on open‑weight models from the Gemma family (Gemma 12B, Gemma 27B, MedGemma‑27B) to avoid transmitting patient data to external servers.

## Experimental Setup
- **Data**: 100 question‑response pairs sampled from 100 k existing consultations, annotated by two company employees.  
- **Metrics**: Empathy and problems addressed scored 1–5 Likert; all other metrics binary.  
- **Models**: Gemma 12B, Gemma 27B, MedGemma‑27B.  
- **Prompt Optimizers**: Labeled Few‑Shot, Bootstrap Few‑Shot, SIMBA.  
- **Evaluation**: Offline scoring against human annotations; self‑evaluation via the Reconciliation Agent; live deployment on a telemedicine platform with 41 doctors, processing 871 evaluation requests.

## Results
| Aspect | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Best Scorer | MedGemma‑27B with SIMBA (highest Pearson for empathy/problems addressed, best F1 for binary metrics) |
| Recommender | MedGemma‑27B |
| Live deployment | 871 requests, 41 doctors |
| Doctor revisions | 120 out of 871 responses revised |
| Quality improvement (live) | 10 % overall relative improvement across all metrics |
| Predicted improvement (self‑eval) | 28 % (Reconciliation Agent) |
| Patient reviews | 47.5 % positive for responses with suggestions vs 25.83 % without |
| Increase in positive reviews | 83.89 % |

The Recommender’s suggestions led to a 10 % real‑world improvement, slightly lower than the 28 % predicted by the self‑evaluation procedure, indicating a bias of the Scorer toward outputs generated by the Reconciliation Agent.

## Industry Impact
Dr.Copilot was deployed in a real telemedicine platform, serving 41 Romanian‑speaking doctors. The system delivered specific feedback on the top three most impactful metrics (ranked by correlation with patient reviews) and achieved a substantial 83.89 % increase in the like‑to‑response ratio, directly translating to higher patient satisfaction and potential business benefits such as improved doctor reputation and platform retention.

## Limitations
- The Scorer Agent shows a bias toward outputs produced by the Reconciliation Agent, as evidenced by the discrepancy between predicted and actual improvements.  
- The dataset is limited to 100 annotated examples, which may constrain generalization.  
- The system focuses solely on presentation quality; medical content correctness is not evaluated.  
- Deployment is confined to Romanian text‑based telemedicine; applicability to other languages or modalities remains untested.
