# Summary

## TL;DR  
The paper presents a deterministic, transparent, and modular framework—**ToolGuards**—for enforcing company policy adherence in LLM‑driven agentic workflows. By compiling free‑form policy documents into verifiable guard code at build time and executing these guards before each tool invocation at runtime, the system guarantees that agents never violate policy‑specified constraints. Preliminary experiments on the τ‑bench Airlines domain (14 tools, naturalistic policies) show that the approach achieves a reference‑level F1 of 0.80 and a policy‑level fuzzy RandIndex of 0.83, outperforming several state‑of‑the‑art LLM baselines.

## Problem & Motivation  
Large Language Model (LLM) agents promise flexible business process automation, yet they struggle to reliably follow complex company policies. Existing solutions append policy documents to the agent’s prompt and rely on a “best‑effort” strategy, which is non‑deterministic, vulnerable to attacks, and does not scale to enterprise settings. Reliable policy adherence is a “make‑or‑break” factor for adopting agentic AI at scale, motivating a deterministic enforcement mechanism.

## Approach  
The proposed approach consists of two phases:

1. **Offline Buildtime**  
   * **Tool‑Policy Mapper** – transforms free‑form policy documents into a structured mapping that links each policy statement to the relevant tool(s). It extracts reference spans, splits policies into fine‑grained units, and generates compliance/violation examples.  
   * **ToolGuard Generator** – first generates a policy‑independent skeleton (data types, tool signatures, empty guard stubs) from the OpenAPI specification using pydantic code generation and Python AST. Then, using a test‑driven development (TDD) loop, it fills in guard implementations guided by the compliance/violation tests derived from the mapper. The process is self‑repairing, incorporating syntactic checks (Microsoft Pyright) and semantic feedback (test pass/fail).

2. **Runtime Integration**  
   * Generated **ToolGuards** are executed before each agent tool invocation. If a planned action violates a policy, the guard blocks the call and prompts the agent to self‑reflect and revise its plan. This ensures that no policy‑violating action is ever executed.

The design is deterministic, transparent, and requires only limited expert intervention (review of mapper output and generated guard code).

## Experimental Setup  
* **Domain & Data** – τ‑bench Airlines (Yao et al., 2024). 14 tools, a large set of naturalistic, real‑world policies.  
* **Mapper Evaluation** – Manual ground‑truth mappings for the 14 tools. Metrics: precision, recall, F1, and fuzzy RandIndex (FRI).  
* **Baselines** – GPT‑4o, GPT‑4.1‑mini, Claude‑Sonnet‑3.5, Llama‑3.3‑70B‑Instruct, Qwen2.5‑72B‑Instruct.  
* **Sensitivity Tests** – Appending out‑of‑domain (OOD) SalesForce retail policies from Huang et al., 2024, and irrelevant in‑domain (IID) policies.  
* **Code Generation** – Over 800 lines of guard code, ~5 K tokens of structured mapping.
