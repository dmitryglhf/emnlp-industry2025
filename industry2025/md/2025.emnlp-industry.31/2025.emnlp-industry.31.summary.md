# Summary

## TL;DR
Wayfair released a production‑grade, LLM‑driven pipeline that first extracts aspect–sentiment pairs from customer reviews, consolidates them into canonical terms, samples representative reviews, and then prompts Gemini 1.5 Flash to generate concise, 300–500‑character product summaries. The system was evaluated offline on 341 products (~50 k reviews) and online in a 3‑week A/B test on 493 208 products, yielding statistically significant gains of +0.3 % in Add‑to‑Cart Rate, +0.5 % in Conversion Rate, and –0.13 % in bounce rate, with no negative impact on revenue or page speed.

## Problem & Motivation
E‑commerce platforms suffer from an overwhelming volume of customer reviews that are repetitive and noisy, making it hard for shoppers to quickly find relevant information. Existing LLM summarization approaches risk hallucination, omission, and misrepresentation, especially when the review set exceeds the model’s context window. The paper addresses the need for a controllable, aspect‑guided summarization that remains faithful to actual customer feedback while fitting within the practical constraints of a production environment.

## Approach
The system is a modular, model‑agnostic pipeline consisting of four stages:

1. **Aspect extraction** – Each review is processed by Gemini 1.5 Flash with a prompt that extracts up to five product aspects and their sentiment labels (positive, mixed, negative) in JSON form.
2. **Aspect consolidation** – A second LLM prompt maps fine‑grained aspects to broader canonical terms. The mapping keeps aspects occurring above the 95th percentile (30 occurrences) unchanged; less frequent aspects are merged. A caching mechanism reuses mappings for new data, ensuring consistent vocabulary over time.
3. **Aspect‑based review selection** – For each product, the top five most frequent aspect–sentiment pairs are identified. A subset of reviews mentioning each pair is sampled, capped at 200 reviews per product. Sampling is weighted by aspect frequency, preserving the relative prominence of opinions; business criteria such as recency or verified purchase can be applied.
4. **Aspect‑guided summarization** – The selected reviews and consolidated aspects are fed into a prompt that instructs Gemini 1.5 Flash to produce a coherent, concise summary (~300–500 characters) that covers the most frequent aspects and reflects the underlying sentiment.

The pipeline is fully LLM‑driven, with prompt templates provided in the appendix.

## Experimental Setup
- **Offline evaluation**: 341 products (≥10 reviews each) totaling ~50 k reviews. Manual annotation used an error taxonomy (no errors, exaggeration/understatement, minor/major misrepresentation, minor/major omission). Inter‑coder agreement was 70 % (majority vote).  
- **Online A/B test**: March 2025, 3 weeks, 493 208 products across 2 329 product classes. The control version displayed standard reviews; the treatment added the aspect‑guided summary and clickable aspect filters. Metrics measured: Add‑to‑Cart Rate (ATCR), Conversion Rate (CVR), bounce rate, Gross Revenue per Session (GRS), and Page Speed Index. All metrics were statistically significant with *p* = 0.10.

## Results
- **Offline**: 285/341 (84 %) summaries had no errors; 33/341 (11 %) had minor problems; 15/341 (5 %) had main issues (exaggeration, major misrepresentation, major omission).  
- **Online**: ATCR increased by **0.3 %**, CVR by **0.5 %**, bounce rate decreased by **0.13 %**. No significant negative effects on GRS or page speed.

## Industry Impact
Wayfair deployed the system across its live e‑commerce platform. The A/B test covered **493 208 products** in **2 329** product classes, demonstrating measurable improvements in key customer engagement metrics (ATCR, CVR, bounce). The deployment strategy includes automatic re‑generation when new reviews reach 10 % of the existing count, ensuring up‑to‑date summaries without manual intervention.

## Limitations
The pipeline’s overall quality hinges on each component’s performance; noisy or inconsistent outputs at any stage can degrade the final summary. The choice of underlying LLM (Gemini 1.5 Flash) is critical, affecting accuracy, fluency, and sentiment alignment. Performance may deteriorate for rare product classes with limited review coverage or highly domain‑specific terminology. Future work should explore robustness across broader domains, languages, and product categories, and improve alignment between extracted aspects and user intent.
