# Summary

## TL;DR  
CLARITY is a hybrid clinical assistant that couples a Finite State Machine (FSM) with a Large Language Model (LLM) – GigaChat) to route patients, generate diagnostic hypotheses, and triage severity. In a two‑month pilot on a national inter‑hospital telehealth platform, it handled over 55 k user dialogues, achieving first‑attempt routing precision that surpasses human performance and cutting consultation time by up to three times.

## Problem & Motivation  
Existing patient routing often requires an intermediary GP visit, inflating wait times and administrative burden. Automated, LLM‑driven triage can streamline access to specialists, reduce costs, and improve resource allocation. However, pure LLMs risk hallucinations, mis‑diagnoses, and safety violations, making them unsuitable for clinical deployment without additional safeguards.

## Approach  
CLARITY’s core idea is a **hybrid architecture**:

1. **FSM‑based dialogue manager** – six states (Initialization, Information Collection, Diagnostic Hypothesis & Routing, Moderation, Emergency, Free Dialogue) that enforce structured, safe interactions.
2. **Microservices** – separate services for text generation (LLM), decision making, moderation, emergency detection, readiness estimation, question detection, information collection, specialty selection, and answer generation.
3. **LLM (GigaChat)** – used for natural‑language generation, diagnostic hypothesis generation, specialist selection, and explanation generation, all guided by a domain‑specific prompt to constrain medical knowledge and ethics.
4. **Hierarchical FSM** – a global graph for overall flow and local graphs for specialty‑specific workflows, enabling linear scalability.

Design choices: rule‑based FSM guarantees safety and predictability; microservices isolate latency; LLM provides flexibility and up‑to‑date knowledge.

## Experimental Setup  
- **Datasets**: 2 500 expert‑annotated medical dialogues (used for training readiness estimator, question detector, etc.).  
- **Baselines**: Not explicitly reported; performance is compared against expert annotations.  
- **Metrics**: Precision, recall, F1, pairwise precision/recall, mean absolute percentage error (MAPE).  
- **Deployment**: Online pilot on a nationwide inter‑hospital telehealth platform; 55 k+ content‑rich user dialogues over two months; 2 500 dialogues were expert‑annotated for validation.

CLARITY was deployed at scale on a national inter‑hospital telehealth platform, processing over 55 k user dialogues in two months. The system was integrated into existing workflows and IT solutions, demonstrating safe, efficient, and robust performance suitable for real‑world clinical settings.
