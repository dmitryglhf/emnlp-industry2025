# Summary

## TL;DR  
HERB is a synthetic benchmark for Deep Search over heterogeneous enterprise data, comprising 39,190 artifacts and 815 answerable queries. Evaluated RAG systems achieve at best an average performance of 32.96, with retrieval identified as the main bottleneck. The benchmark is released on GitHub and HuggingFace to stress‑test enterprise‑oriented LLMs and RAG pipelines.

## Problem & Motivation  
Enterprise RAG tasks demand *deep search*—determining *what* to search for and *where* to search—across diverse sources such as documents, Slack, GitHub PRs, and URLs. Existing multihop benchmarks (e.g., HotpotQA, MultiHop‑RAG) build queries over clusters of related documents linked by explicit entities or topical similarity, producing artificial, shallow reasoning. These approaches fail to capture real‑world enterprise workflows, where evidence is dispersed across structured and unstructured formats and often hidden behind human‑to‑human interactions. HERB addresses this gap by generating realistic enterprise queries and the corresponding evidence, enabling evaluation of RAG systems in a realistic enterprise context.

## Approach  
HERB adopts a *query‑first* data synthesis strategy.  
1. **Enterprise Schema** – A simplified software company with 530 employees, 30 products, and six functional orgs (Slack, Sales, Einstein, Salesforce, Tableau, Mulesoft).  
2. **Query Templates** – 41 templates (12 content‑based, 14 people‑search, 8 artifact‑search, 7 customer‑search) manually designed by domain experts, each with a deterministic answer format.  
3. **Workflows** – Three core stages (planning, development, deployment) each contain three realistic workflows, yielding 9 total workflows. Workflows simulate meetings, Slack threads, GitHub PRs, documents, and URLs, producing 302 Slack channels, 33,632 messages, 400 documents, 3,562 PRs, 575 URLs, 321 meeting transcripts, 50 meeting chats, and 120 customer profiles.  
4. **Evidence Generation** – LLMs generate artifacts conditioned on the chosen workflow, ensuring that each query is supported by a subset of artifacts while other artifacts act as distractors.  
5. **Answer Generation** – For each query, a structured, query‑specific inference process aggregates relevant artifacts, resolves entity disambiguation, and produces the final answer.  
6. **Unanswerable Queries** – 699 queries are paired with workflows lacking the required evidence, mirroring UAEval4RAG.

## Experimental Setup  
- **Dataset**: HERB (39,190 artifacts, 815 answerable queries, 699 unanswerable queries).  
- **Baselines & Configurations**:  
  - Zero‑shot prompting (no retrieval)  
  - Vector retriever (textembedding‑3‑large)  
  - Hybrid vector + BM25 retriever  
  - Raptor  
  - GraphRAG (GRAG)  
  - HippoRAG 2 (HRAG)  
  - Proposition‑Graph RAG (PGRAG)  
  - Agentic RAG using ReAct, GPT‑4o for generation, and textembedding‑3‑large for vectorization.  
- **Evaluation Metric**: Average performance score (higher is better).  
- **Deployment Context**: Offline evaluation on the HERB dataset; no online A/B‑test reported.

## Results  
| System | Avg. Performance |
|--------|------------------|
| Hybrid vector + BM25 | **20.61** |
| Agentic RAG (ReAct) | **32.96** |

- Retrieval is the main bottleneck: existing methods often retrieve only partial evidence, leading to significant performance degradation.  
- Even the best agentic RAG achieves only 32.96, indicating substantial room for improvement.  
- No production or A/B‑test results are reported.

## Industry Impact  
Not reported.

## Limitations  
- Retrieval remains the primary bottleneck; best baseline only 20.61, agentic only 32.96.  
- The dataset is synthetic; while it simulates realistic workflows, it may not fully capture the nuances of real enterprise data.  
- No real‑world deployment or business outcome is documented.
