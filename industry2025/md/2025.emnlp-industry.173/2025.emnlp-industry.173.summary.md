# Summary

## TL;DR
The paper presents a cross‑modal knowledge distillation framework that transfers both textual and visual knowledge from large teacher models (LLMs and VLMs) into a lightweight, domain‑specific retriever. Trained on shipbuilding and electrical‑equipment PDFs, the distilled retriever outperforms larger baselines (e.g., ColQwen2.5‑3B) while achieving the lowest latency and FLOPs, making it suitable for industrial RAG deployments.

## Problem & Motivation
Industrial RAG systems require retrievers that understand domain‑specific terminology and document‑structural elements (DSE) such as tables, figures, charts, and formulas. Conventional retrievers, even when fine‑tuned, struggle with these DSE and demand expensive expert‑annotated data. Visual retrievers can capture DSE but incur high computational cost, hindering real‑world deployment. The paper therefore asks: *How can we build an efficient retriever that adapts to a specific domain while capturing DSE?*

## Approach
1. **Query Generation (LLM‑based)** – An LLM (Qwen3‑32B) receives a positive PDF page, cropped visual elements, and hard negatives, and generates a synthetic query that is highly relevant to the positive but unrelated to the negatives.  
2. **Ranking Score Generation (VLM‑based)** – A VLM (ColQwen2.5‑3B) scores the relevance of the query to the positive and negative images, producing fine‑grained visual‑semantic scores that reflect layout, figures, and other DSE.  
3. **Knowledge Distillation** – A student dense retriever (BGE‑M3 or Qwen3‑Embedding‑0.6B) is trained with contrastive loss on the synthetic query‑document pairs and a KL‑divergence loss that aligns its predicted relevance distribution with the VLM‑derived scores (Eq. 1). This joint objective teaches the retriever both semantic relevance and structural awareness.

## Experimental Setup
| Item | Details |
|------|---------|
| **Datasets** | Shipbuilding: train 6,625 samples (10 PDFs), test 2,932 samples (9 PDFs). Electrical: train 4,109 samples (32 PDFs), test 3,312 samples (36 PDFs). |
| **Additional Test** | 100 shipbuilding queries covering 5 multimodal types (figures, tables, formulas, charts, plain text). |
| **Baselines** | OCR‑based: BGE‑M3, GTE‑Qwen2‑instruct, Qwen3‑Embedding. Visual: GME, ColPali, ColQwen2.5. Commercial: Google text‑embedding‑005, text‑embedding‑large‑exp‑03‑07. |
| **Backbones** | Student retrievers: BGE‑M3 (560 M) and Qwen3‑Embedding‑0.6B (596 M). Teacher VLM: ColQwen2.5‑3B (3 B). |
| **Evaluation Metrics** | Retrieval: MRR, nDCG, HIT. RAG: faithfulness, answer correctness (via RAGAs). |
| **Efficiency** | Latency and FLOPs normalized to 1.0 (BGE‑M3). |

*Key observations*  
- The distilled retrievers surpass all baselines across every metric.  
- They achieve the best performance with the smallest parameter count (560 M).  
- Latency and FLOPs are an order of magnitude lower than the teacher VLM (ColQwen2.5‑3B).  
- In RAG, the distilled retriever yields faithfulness and answer correctness comparable to the teacher retriever, while other RAG variants (M3DocRAG, VisdomRAG) lag significantly.
