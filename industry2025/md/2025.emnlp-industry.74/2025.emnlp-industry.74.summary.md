# Summary

## TL;DR  
REIC introduces a Retrieval‑augmented Generation (RAG) framework for intent classification that dynamically incorporates relevant knowledge via dense vector retrieval and constrained decoding. On a real‑world customer‑service dataset (52,499 training samples, 3,364 test samples split across 1P and 3P verticals), REIC outperforms fine‑tuned RoBERTa, Mistral‑based classifiers, and Claude zero/few‑shot prompting, achieving an overall F1 of **0.572**. The approach scales to thousands of intents without retraining, making it suitable for industry‑level intent routing.

## Problem & Motivation  
Accurate intent classification is essential for routing customer queries to the most appropriate agents, directly impacting handling time and operational cost. As e‑commerce platforms expand product lines, the number of intents grows and taxonomies diverge across verticals (e.g., 1P device troubleshooting vs. 3P physical order issues). Existing flat‑label classifiers struggle with scalability and ignore label correlations, leading to misclassifications and costly retraining when new intents appear. The paper targets these challenges by proposing a hierarchical, retrieval‑driven approach that can adapt to evolving intent spaces.

## Approach  
REIC’s pipeline consists of three stages:

1. **Index Construction** – A dense vector index is built from a held‑out annotated set. Each query is encoded with a pre‑trained sentence transformer; the corresponding intent label is stored alongside the embedding.  
2. **Candidate Retrieval** – For a new query \(q\), the same encoder produces vector \(v_q\). Approximate nearest‑neighbor search retrieves the top‑\(k\) most similar (query, intent) pairs \(E\) using cosine similarity.  
3. **Intent Probability Calculation** – A fine‑tuned Mistral‑7B‑Instructv0.2 LLM performs constrained decoding over the retrieved candidates. For each unique intent \(t_j\) in \(E\), the model outputs a probability \(P(t_j|q,E)\). The final intent is the one with the highest probability.  
The constrained decoding masks all tokens except the intent sequence, mitigating hallucination and ensuring that only retrieved intents are considered. This design allows dynamic updates to the intent space by simply adding new (query, intent) pairs to the index, without retraining the LLM.

## Experimental Setup  
| Component | Details |
|-----------|----------|
| **Dataset** | 52,499 training samples (35,041 1P, 17,458 3P); test set 3,647 1P + 1,717 3P |
| **Baselines** | RoBERTa‑base (multi‑head), Mistral‑7B‑v0.3 (sequence‑classification head), Claude 3.5 Sonnet (zero‑shot, few‑shot, + RAG) |
| **Metrics** | Precision, Recall, F1 per vertical and overall |
| **Retrievers** | BM25, MPNet, Contriever‑MS MACRO, ColBERT‑v2 |
| **LLM** | Mistral‑7B‑Instructv0.2 fine‑tuned with LoRA (rank 8, alpha 16, dropout 0.1) |
| **Training** | 8 NVIDIA‑A100 40 GB GPUs, 96 vCPUs, batch 8, LR 2e‑5, 3 epochs (~3 h) |
| **Loss** | Cross‑Entropy |

## Results  
### Intent Detection (Table 1)  
| Model | 3P Precision | 3P Recall | 3P F1 | 1P Precision | 1P Recall | 1P F1 | Overall Precision | Overall Recall | Overall F1 |
|-------|---------------|------------|--------|---------------|------------|--------|-------------------|----------------|-----------|
| RoBERTa | 0.527 | 0.447 | 0.483 | 0.583 | 0.488 | 0.531 | 0.565 | 0.474 | 0.516 |
| Mistral Classification | 0.215 | 0.228 | 0.221 | 0.301 | 0.250 | 0.273 | 0.269 | 0.243 | 0.255 |
| Claude Zero‑shot | 0.338 | 0.250 | 0.287 | 0.238 | 0.170 | 0.199 | 0.271 | 0.196 | 0.227 |
| Claude Few‑shot | 0.386 | 0.289 | 0.331 | 0.350 | 0.308 | 0.328 | 0.361 | 0.302 | 0.329 |
| Claude + RAG | 0.473 | 0.438 | 0.455 | 0.415 | 0.389 | 0.402 | 0.434 | 0.404 | 0.419 |

REIC improves overall F1 by **+0.153** over the best baseline (Claude + RAG).
