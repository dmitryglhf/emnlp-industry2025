# Summary

## TL;DR  
The paper introduces a zero‑annotation pipeline that turns automotive dashboard diagrams into Spatial Knowledge Graphs (SKGs) using Large Vision‑Language Models (LVLMs). Three generation strategies—Per‑Attribute, Per‑Component, and Single‑Prompt—are compared on a new 65‑vehicle dataset (995 components, 15,852 relationships). The Per‑Component pipeline with Gemini 2.5 Flash (excluding the Frequency attribute) yields the best SKG, achieving 71.3 % agreement with human‑selected landmarks. The SKG is then used to answer location‑based questions more intuitively than an image‑only baseline.

## Problem & Motivation  
Drivers often receive unhelpful spatial answers such as “It is to the right of the Lane‑Keeping Assist button” when they do not know that button. Existing LVLMs struggle to identify salient landmarks and precise spatial relations in monochrome technical diagrams. The paper tackles two gaps: (1) selecting effective landmarks from a diagram’s legend, and (2) extracting accurate spatial relationships from complex diagrams. The goal is to provide intuitive, landmark‑based answers for in‑car virtual assistants.

## Approach  
1. **Graph Generation Pipelines**  
   * **Per‑Attribute** – separate LVLM prompts for each of four attributes (Size, Frequency, Proximity, Position).  
   * **Per‑Component** – a single prompt per component that outputs all attributes and relationships to other components.  
   * **Single‑Prompt** – one comprehensive prompt that asks the LVLM to output the entire graph at once (only feasible with Gemini 2.5 Flash).  
2. **SKG Structure** – directed graph \(G=(V,E)\) where vertices are components enriched with attributes (Size, Frequency, Proximity, Position) and edges encode spatial relations (Proximity, Position).  
3. **Significance Score** – rule‑based metric combining attribute scores:  
   \[
   \text{Score}=\frac{S_{\text{size}}+S_{\text{freq}}+S_{\text{prox}}+S_{\text{pos}}}{4}
   \]  
   (Equation 1) or, excluding Frequency,  
   \[
   \text{Score}=\frac{S_{\text{size}}+S_{\text{prox}}+S_{\text{pos}}}{3}
   \]  
   (Equation 2).  
   The top‑3 components per node by this score become the landmarks used for QA.  
4. **Attribute‑to‑Score Mapping** – Table 1 maps categorical values to numeric scores (e.g., VERY_LARGE → 1.0, SMALL → 0.4).  
5. **Positional Centrality** – Position attribute is converted to a spatial centrality score based on normalized inverse distance from the diagram’s geometric center (Algorithm 1 in Appendix D).

## Experimental Setup  
* **Dataset** – 65 vehicles from 9 manufacturers, 995 components, 15,852 relationships (publicly available at https://github.com/steve-bakos/vehicle-components-dataset).  
* **Baselines & Models** – GPT‑4o and Gemini 2.5 Flash.  
* **Evaluation Stages** – (1) attribute accuracy per pipeline, (2) landmark agreement with human annotators, (3) downstream QA performance.  
* **QA Setup** – Two systems:  
  * **Image‑Only Baseline** – LVLM receives image + question, generates answer directly.  
  * **KG‑Augmented** – LLM first identifies target component, then queries the pre‑computed SKG, retrieves relevant triplets, and generates an answer.  
* **Questions** – 10 curated questions per diagram, ground‑truth answers manually authored using human‑annotated landmarks.  
* **Metrics** – BLEU‑4, ROUGE‑1, BERTScore, and an LLM‑based judge that scores correct spatial relations (1 hit → 0.5, 2 hits → 0.75, 3 hits → 1).

## Results  
| Pipeline | Model | Agreement (%) |
|----------|-------|----------------|
| Per‑Attribute | GPT‑4o | 45.0 |
| Per‑Attribute | Gemini 2.5 Flash | 51.0 |
| Per‑Component | GPT‑4o | 57.1 |
| Per‑Component | GPT‑4o (No Freq.) | 69.7 |
| Per‑Component | Gemini 2.5 Flash | 52.6 |
| Per‑Component | Gemini 2.5 Flash (No Freq.) | **71.3** |
| Single‑Prompt | Gemini 2.5 Flash | 41.2 |
| Single‑Prompt | Gemini 2.5 Flash (No Freq.) | 54.3 |

*Per‑Component with Gemini 2.5 Flash (excluding Frequency) achieves the highest landmark agreement (71.3 %).*  
Per‑Attribute performs best on purely visual attributes (Position, Size) when using Gemini 2.5 Flash (Table 3, Appendix A).  
Single‑Prompt baseline is generally the least accurate.  
Downstream QA metrics (BLEU‑4, ROUGE‑1, BERTScore, LLM‑judge) are reported but specific numeric values are not provided in the paper.

## Limitations  
* The Single‑Prompt baseline is less accurate, indicating token‑window constraints limit full‑graph generation.  
* The Per‑Attribute pipeline trades off contextual richness, yielding lower landmark agreement.  
* The dataset is limited to 65 vehicles across 9 manufacturers; generalization to other diagram styles remains untested.  
* Evaluation uses only GPT‑4o and Gemini 2.5 Flash; performance with other LVLMs is unknown.  
* The approach requires offline LVLM inference for graph generation, which may be costly at scale.  
* The Significance score is rule‑based; its effectiveness depends on the chosen attribute mappings and may not capture all human landmark preferences.
