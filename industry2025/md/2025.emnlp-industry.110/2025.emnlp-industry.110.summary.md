# Summary

## TL;DR  
The paper introduces **ASR‑EC**, the first public benchmark for Chinese ASR error correction, built from THCHS‑30, AISHELL‑1/2, and WeNetSpeech using Kaldi‑K1 and Kaldi‑K2 pipelines. It evaluates three LLM‑based strategies—prompting, parameter‑efficient fine‑tuning (LoRA), and multimodal augmentation (audio + text)—on ChatGLM3, Qwen, and Baichuan2. Experiments show that prompting is ineffective, fine‑tuning helps only for some models, and multimodal augmentation yields the best character‑error‑rate (CER) reductions, achieving state‑of‑the‑art performance on the benchmark.

## Problem & Motivation  
Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR) still suffers from errors caused by noise, ambiguity, etc. While English ASR‑error‑correction datasets exist, **no Chinese benchmark had been available**. The authors therefore build ASR‑EC to enable research on Chinese ASR error correction and to assess how large language models (LLMs) can be adapted to this task.

## Approach  
1. **Dataset Construction** – Four open‑source corpora (THCHS‑30, AISHELL‑1, AISHELL‑2, WeNetSpeech) are processed with Kaldi‑K1 (DNN‑HMM hybrid) and Kaldi‑K2 (Zipformer‑Transducer) to generate erroneous transcripts. Utterances are split into *short* (≈13 chars) and *long* (≈38 chars) subsets; only 10 % of utterances with CER = 0 are kept.  
2. **LLM Paradigms**  
   * **Prompting** – zero‑shot, few‑shot, and multi‑step prompting (two‑step: detect errors → correct).  
   * **Fine‑tuning** – parameter‑efficient LoRA fine‑tuning of ChatGLM3, Qwen, and Baichuan2.  
   * **Multimodal Augmentation** – audio and text are encoded separately (Qianwen‑audio encoders), concatenated, and fed to the LLM. The model is trained end‑to‑end on the ASR‑EC data.  
3. **Evaluation** – CER (character‑error‑rate) is the primary metric; baselines are the original Kaldi‑K1 (for ASR‑EC A*) and Kaldi‑K2 (for ASR‑EC B*).

## Experimental Setup  
| Model | Parameters | Modality |
|-------|------------|----------|
| ChatGLM3 | 6 B | Text |
| Qwen | 7 B | Text |
| Baichuan2 | 7 B | Text |

Generation settings: ChatGLM3 uses a repetition penalty of 1.05 (default 1) to reduce repetition; Qwen and Baichuan2 use default configs. LoRA hyper‑parameters are taken from the official repositories.  
Training data: 544,551 utterances (186,150 short + 358,400 long).  
Test data: 1,024 utterances (512 short + 512 long).  
Baseline CERs:  
- **ASR‑EC A*** (Kaldi‑K1): 13.49 % (short), 12.24 % (long), 12.45 % (whole).  
- **ASR‑EC B*** (Kaldi‑K2): 12.99 % (short), 7.03 % (long), 8.15 % (whole).

## Results  
| Dataset | Method | Test CER (short) | Test CER (long) | Test CER (whole) |
|---------|--------|-------------------|-----------------|------------------|
| ASR‑EC A* | Baseline (Kaldi‑K1) | 13.49 | 12.24 | 12.45 |
| ASR‑EC A* | Multimodal | 12.83 | 11.73 | 12.42 |
| ASR‑EC B* | Baseline (Kaldi‑K2) | 12.99 | 7.03 | 8.15 |
| ASR‑EC B* | Multimodal | 13.44 | 6.92 | 8.11 |

Error‑type CERs (overall) for multimodal:  
- **ASR‑EC A***: 13.32 %  
- **ASR‑EC B***: 10.39 %  

Prompting produced no significant improvement and sometimes introduced new errors. Fine‑tuning helped only for certain LLMs (e.g., Baichuan2) but did not outperform multimodal augmentation. The multimodal approach achieved the lowest CERs, especially on long utterances, confirming that joint audio‑text reasoning is essential for Chinese ASR error correction.
