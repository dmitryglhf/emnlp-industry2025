# Summary

## TL;DR  
The paper introduces a production‑ready talent‑search ranking system that (1) uses an LLM to extract fine‑grained job preferences from job descriptions and historical hiring data, (2) employs a role‑aware multi‑gate MoE (MMoE) network to capture recruiter‑role heterogeneity, and (3) jointly optimizes CTR, CVR, and resume relevance in a multi‑task framework.  The system achieves 1.70 % relative AUC gain on CTR, 5.97 % on CVR, and a 17.29 % lift in click‑through conversion rate (CTCVR) in an online A/B test, translating into millions of CNY in annual cost savings by reducing reliance on external sourcing channels.

## Problem & Motivation  
Talent search on online recruitment platforms must rank candidates for recruiter queries while capturing nuanced job‑specific preferences and recruiter behavior. Existing methods fall short because they (i) ignore fine‑grained job signals, (ii) treat all recruiters uniformly (missing role‑aware personalization), and (iii) are noisy due to subjective judgments and expertise gaps.  The paper targets a real‑world platform where recruiters (SA, SG, TL) generate clicks and application initiations, and improving CTR/CVR directly reduces external channel fees.

## Approach  
The system has three core components:

1. **Encoding Module** – recruiter, query, and talent IDs are embedded via lookup tables.  A fine‑grained JD encoder uses an LLM with chain‑of‑thought prompts to summarize job preferences from the JD text and historical resumes.  The resulting JD and resume embeddings are fused with a Gated Cross Network (GCN) and further enriched by multi‑head attention over the recruiter’s historical interactions.

2. **Personalized MMoE Module** – a role‑aware multi‑gate MoE network routes the concatenated vector  
   \[
   x = [e^{(r)}; e^{(q)}; e^{(t)}; e^{(j)}]
   \]  
   through multiple expert networks.  A role‑embedding‑driven gating network produces a softmax distribution \(\{g_i\}\) that weights each expert’s output, yielding \(\hat{x}\).  Four gating networks are used: three for the primary tasks (CTR, CVR, relevance) and one shared for cross‑task representation.

3. **Multi‑Task Learning Module** – CTR, CVR, and resume relevance are formulated as binary classification tasks.  A shared expert captures common features, while task‑specific DNN towers receive the shared output plus the relevance signal.  Final predictions are produced by linear projections followed by softmax, and the total loss is a weighted sum of cross‑entropy losses.

## Experimental Setup  
- **Dataset** – Proprietary online recruitment logs:  
  * Training: ~495,000 samples from ~1,000 recruiters, 200,000 talent profiles, 500 job postings.  
  * Test: ~257,000 samples collected over a subsequent three‑week period.  
- **Baselines** – Single‑task: **GDCN**.  Multi‑task: **ESSM**, **MMoE**, **PLE**, **STEM** (state‑of‑the‑art).  
- **Metrics** – AUC, MRR@10, AP for CTR and CVR; online metric **CTCVR** (applications per impression).  
- **Implementation** – Hyperparameters and training protocols are detailed in Appendix D.  
- **Online Evaluation** – 1:1 traffic split, 9 consecutive working days, Google standard experimentation protocol; baseline is **GDCN**.

## Results  
| Method | CTR AUC | CTR MRR@10 | CTR AP | CVR AUC | CVR MRR@10 | CVR AP |
|--------|---------|------------|--------|----------|------------|--------|
| GDCN | 0.6456 | 0.0131 | 0.2816 | 0.6389 | 0.0155 | 0.0484 |
| ESSM | 0.7067 | 0.0134 | 0.2988 | 0.7005 | 0.0163 | 0.0633 |
| MMoE | 0.7106 | 0.0130 | 0.3010 | 0.7108 | 0.0152 | 0.0644 |
| PLE | 0.6959 | 0.0132 | 0.2974 | 0.6884 | 0.0144 | 0.0624 |
| STEM | 0.7091 | 0.0132 | 0.3038 | 0.7154 | 0.0152 | 0.0613 |
| **Ours** | **0.7227** | **0.0136** | **0.3139** | **0.7581** | **0.0192** | **0.0680** |

- **Relative Gains** – 1.70 % (CTR) and 5.97 % (CVR) over the best baseline.  
- **Online A/B Test** – 17.29 % relative improvement in CTCVR; p‑value = 0.01598 (< 0.05).  
- **Business Impact** – 40 % of hires already come from the internal talent database; the lift in CTCVR further reduces reliance on external channels (e.g., headhunting), yielding millions of CNY in annual cost savings.

## Industry Impact  
The system is deployed at a production‑scale recruitment platform (Alibaba Group).  In the online A/B test, the experimental group (our model) outperformed the baseline **GDCN** over nine working days with a 1:1 traffic split.  The improvement in CTCVR directly translates to higher internal hire rates and lower external channel fees, demonstrating tangible business value.
