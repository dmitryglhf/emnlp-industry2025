# Summary

## TL;DR  
The paper introduces a privacy‑compliant, scalable framework for synthesizing high‑quality doctor‑patient dialogues. By combining **Intent Graph Planning**, **Dual‑Agent Simulation** (Doctor/Patient agents powered by DeepSeek‑V3), and a **Rule‑Reward Quality Control** module, the system generates realistic, intent‑consistent, and medically faithful conversations that substantially improve downstream EMR generation, reducing clinician editing effort.

## Problem & Motivation  
Real‑world electronic medical record (EMR) generation requires large volumes of realistic doctor‑patient dialogues. Existing public corpora are short, task‑focused, and lack colloquial disfluencies, while template‑based or naive language‑model sampling produces rigid, repetitive, and medically inconsistent dialogues. This gap limits the ability of EMR models to generalize to real consultations, leading to factual inaccuracies and increased physician editing. The paper addresses this bottleneck by proposing a framework that can generate long, diverse, and clinically faithful dialogues at scale.

## Approach  
The framework consists of three core components:

1. **Intent Graph Planning** –  
   * Extracts a fine‑grained intent library from 617 real consultations (7 departments).  
   * Builds a directed graph \(\mathcal{G}=(\mathcal{V},\mathcal{E})\) where nodes are intents (speaker role + communicative act).  
   * Computes transition probabilities \(P(v_j|v_i)\) and samples intent skeletons via Algorithm 1 (SampleIntentSequence).  
   * Allows dynamic adaptation to patient attributes (age, chronic conditions).

2. **Dual‑Agent Simulation** –  
   * Two agents (Doctor and Patient) alternate turns.  
   * Each agent receives context \(C_t=(v_t,H_{1:t-1},\mathcal{P},\mathcal{K})\).  
   * Uses nucleus sampling (top‑p, \(T=1.0\)) to generate multiple candidates \(\mathcal{U}_t\).  
   * Agents are both instantiated with DeepSeek‑V3; role‑specific prompting ensures authoritative doctor utterances and colloquial patient responses.

3. **Rule‑Reward Quality Control** –  
   * Hard rule filtering (Appendix A.8) enforces speaker role, medical terminology, length, and intent alignment.  
   * Reward model \(R_{\phi}\) trained via self‑supervised preference learning (Equation 3) on ~20 k dialogue pairs, fine‑tuned on Qwen‑2.5‑14B.  
   * Final utterance \(u_t^*\) is the rule‑passing candidate with highest reward (Equation 6).  
   * If no candidate passes, the system regenerates with adjusted sampling until a valid utterance is produced.

## Experimental Setup  
* **Dataset** – 617 real consultations (average 66 ± 54 turns) from a tertiary Chinese hospital across 7 departments.  
* **Splits** – 500 training, 117 test (no patient overlap).  
* **Baselines** –  
  * **Real‑only** (EMR trained only on real dialogues).  
  * **Single‑Agent (No Control)** – one agent without intent guidance or quality control.  
  * **Dual‑Agent (No Control)** – two agents without intent planning or control modules.  
* **Evaluation Metrics** –  
  * Dialogue quality: Turns/Dialogue, Rule Pass (%), Illegal (%), Tokens/Turn, Dist‑1, Dist‑2, Entropy.  
  * EMR downstream: BLEU, Key Information Error (KeyErr %), Edit Count, LLM Judge (GPT‑40, 5‑point fluency/correctness).  
* **Implementation** – Agents: DeepSeek‑V3; Reward model: Qwen‑2.5‑14B fine‑tuned (batch 32, lr 1e‑5, early stopping patience 3). GPU cluster with NVIDIA A800.
