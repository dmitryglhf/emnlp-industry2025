# Summary

## TL;DR
The paper presents a data‑driven labeling framework that uses large language models (LLMs) to automatically generate training labels for two critical components of a multi‑turn Retrieval‑Augmented Generation (RAG) chatbot: adaptive retrieval and query reformulation.  The proposed “explanation‑guided” labeling strategy for adaptive retrieval and the “keyword‑based” reformulation strategy for queries achieve the best performance, enabling a production‑ready RAG system that can be trained on millions of real customer‑agent transcripts without manual annotation.

## Problem & Motivation
Customer‑service chatbots must decide when to fetch external knowledge and how to rewrite conversational queries into effective search terms.  Existing solutions rely on costly, hand‑labeled data for these two components, which is scarce in real‑world settings.  The paper tackles this bottleneck by automatically generating high‑quality labels from existing customer‑agent dialogues, thereby making multi‑turn RAG systems viable at scale.

## Approach
1. **Data Preparation** – Merge consecutive utterances from the same role into single units, producing dialogues \(d=[q_1,a_1,\dots,q_n,a_n]\).  For each customer query \(q_i\), the conversation history is \(C_i=[q_1,a_1,\dots,q_{i-1},a_{i-1}]\).

2. **Adaptive Retrieval Labeling** – Two LLM prompt strategies:
   * **Intent‑guided**: The LLM first classifies the query into one of 12 predefined intents (Table 7) before deciding whether retrieval is needed.
   * **Explanation‑guided**: The LLM freely explains its decision and outputs a binary label \(p_s\in\{0,1\}\).
   Both strategies are evaluated in *turn‑level* and *dialogue‑level* annotation modes; dialogue‑level consistently yields higher accuracy.

3. **Query Reformulation Labeling** – Two LLM prompt strategies:
   * **NLQ Reformulation**: The LLM rewrites the query into a self‑contained natural‑language question, resolving ellipsis and coreference, and explains the edits.
   * **Keyword Reformulation**: The LLM extracts a concise set of keywords that capture the query intent, also providing an explanation.

4. **Model Training** –  
   * Adaptive retrieval: RoBERTa‑base (cross‑entropy, Adam, lr = 2e‑6).  
   * Query reformulation: BART‑base or FLAN‑T5‑base (cross‑entropy, Adam, lr = 1e‑5).  
   Models are trained for 10 epochs on 4 × NVIDIA A10 GPUs, with batch sizes 30 (retrieval) and 10 (reformulation).  Early stopping (patience = 5) is used.

## Experimental Setup
| Item | Details |
|------|----------|
| **Dataset** | 10,000 synthetic dialogues (20 product categories, avg. 12 customer + 22 agent utterances). |
| **Split** | 70 % train / 10 % validation / 20 % test. |
| **LLM for labeling** | Claude‑Sonnet‑3 (Claude 3.5 Sonnet). |
| **Baselines** | RoBERTa‑base (adaptive retrieval), BART‑base / FLAN‑T5‑base (query reformulation). |
| **Metrics** | Accuracy, AUC (adaptive retrieval); BLEU, METEOR, ROUGE‑1/2/L (query reformulation); human evaluation (grammatical correctness, context carry‑over completeness/accuracy). |
| **Hardware** | 4 × NVIDIA A10 GPUs. |

## Results
### Adaptive Retrieval
| Strategy | Accuracy (LLM‑label) | Accuracy (Human‑label) | AUC (LLM‑label) | AUC (Human‑label) |
|----------|----------------------|------------------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Intent‑guided | 83.27 % | 74.27 % | 92.49 % | 86.16 % |
| Explanation‑guided | **86.49 %** | **83.39 %** | **93.76 %** | **92.00 %** |

*Dialogue‑level annotation* outperforms turn‑level (Table 1: 92 % vs 89 % for explanation‑guided).  
The explanation‑guided strategy yields the highest accuracy and AUC on both synthetic and human‑annotated test sets.

### Query Reformulation
| Reformulation | Model | BLEU | METEOR | ROUGE‑1 | ROUGE‑2 | ROUGE‑L |
|---------------|-------|------|--------|---------|---------|---------|
| NLP | BART‑base | 0.312 | 0.548 | 0.547 | 0.373 | 0.517 |
| NLP | FLAN‑T5‑base | 0.305 | 0.546 | 0.550 | 0.372 | 0.520 |
| Keywords | BART‑base | 0.209 | 0.511 | 0.590 | 0.315 | 0.518 |
| Keywords | FLAN‑T5‑base | 0.204 | 0.501 | 0.592 | 0.314 | 0.520 |

Keyword reformulation scores higher ROUGE‑1 (better keyword coverage) but lower BLEU/METEOR, indicating less fluent but more concise outputs.  Human evaluation shows that keyword reformulations still preserve intent.
