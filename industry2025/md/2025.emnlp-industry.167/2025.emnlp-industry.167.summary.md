# Summary

## TL;DR  
The paper introduces **Politi‑Fact‑Only (PFO)**, a 5‑class political fact‑checking benchmark that removes post‑claim analysis and annotator cues to eliminate leakage.  It also proposes **Recon‑Answer‑Verify (RAV)**, an agentic framework that decomposes verification into structured question‑answer pairs, achieving a 57.5 % macro‑F1 gain on PFO and a 3.01 % gain on the HOVER dataset compared to state‑of‑the‑art baselines.

## Problem & Motivation  
Human fact‑checking cannot keep pace with the volume of misinformation.  Existing benchmarks suffer from *leakage* (post‑claim analysis or annotator commentary) and *incomplete evidence*, which inflate model performance and reduce realism.  The authors aim to provide a leakage‑free dataset and a more robust evaluation framework that reflects real‑time verification scenarios.

## Approach  
### Politi‑Fact‑Only (PFO)  
- Derived from 21 k instances of the PolitiFact dataset.  
- 3 k instances were randomly sampled (600 per class) and manually filtered to remove verdict‑related statements and annotator commentary.  
- Final size: **2 982** instances, evenly split into 5 classes: *true*, *mostly‑true*, *half‑true*, *mostly‑false*, *false*.  
- Evidence token length reduced by **17.79 %** on average (Table 1).  

### Recon‑Answer‑Verify (RAV)  
- Three‑stage pipeline:  
  1. **QGagent** – generates a *verification* or *inquiry* question (single factual triple or entity/relationship).  
  2. **AGagent** – answers the question using the evidence.  
  3. **LGagent** – aggregates the QA history and predicts the final veracity label.  
- Iteration capped at **10** steps; performance plateaus beyond 10 (Appendix Fig. 8).  
- Structured questions reduce cognitive load and improve interpretability.

* RAV achieves a **57.5 %** macro‑F1 improvement on PFO and a **3.01 %** improvement on HOVER compared to ProgramFC.  
* RAV’s macro‑F1 drops only **7.74 %** on the unfiltered PFO, whereas HiSS drops **62.59 %** and ZS drops **44.72 %**.  

**Error Analysis** (AVERITEC, no‑evidence setting):  
- QGagent coverage: **37.6 %**, relevance: **82.6 %**.  
- AGagent answer accuracy: **42.0 %**.  
- Overall macro‑F1: **0.39** vs baseline **0.17**.  

**Reasoning Complexity** (Hover):  
- 2‑hop claims → 4 sub‑questions on average.  
- 3‑hop claims → 5 sub‑questions.  
- 4‑hop claims → 6 sub‑questions.
