# Summary

## TL;DR  
The paper introduces a **batch‑wise alignment strategy** that fine‑tunes large language models (LLMs) on semantically equivalent multilingual data. By forcing the model to generate consistent outputs for the same topic in multiple languages within a single training batch, the authors reduce English‑centric reasoning biases and achieve up to **23.9 %** higher accuracy on non‑English RAG tasks without hurting English performance. The method is lightweight, requires no extra parameters, and integrates seamlessly into existing LLM pipelines.

## Problem & Motivation  
Enterprise applications such as customer support, content moderation, and information retrieval demand **consistent multilingual performance**. Existing LLMs exhibit a large accuracy gap (up to **29 %**) between high‑resource (English) and mid/low‑resource languages because pre‑training data is English‑centric and internal reasoning tends to default to English. This inconsistency erodes user trust and limits operational reliability in global deployments.

## Approach  
* **Batch Alignment** – Each training batch contains *k* samples of the same topic, each in a different language (Arabic, Spanish, French, Japanese, Portuguese, Chinese, and English).  
* **Dataset Construction** – 500 business‑related documents, each translated by humans into the six non‑English languages. Two queries per topic generate *positive* (one relevant document + nine irrelevant) and *negative* (ten irrelevant) samples, yielding 2,000 samples per language.  
* **Fine‑tuning Objectives** – Preference‑based objectives (Direct Preference Optimization, DPO, and Odds‑Ratio Preference Optimization, ORPO) are applied to the batch‑aligned data. The only change from standard fine‑tuning is the batch composition; no extra critics, reward models, or inference overhead are introduced.  

## Experimental Setup  
* **Controlled Retrieval‑Augmented Generation (RAG)** – Retrieval component is fixed and identical across languages; the same set of documents is used for every query.  
* **Instruction Control** – The instruction/query template is always in English; only the documents are localized.  
* **Dataset Split** – 70 % training / 30 % testing, balanced across positive/negative samples.  
* **Metrics** – Primary metric is **accuracy** (binary “Yes, answer is present” / “I don’t know”). 95 % bootstrap confidence intervals (B = 1000) are reported; McNemar’s test (p < 0.01) is used for pairwise accuracy comparisons.  
* **Baselines** – Pre‑trained models (Mixtral‑8x22B, Llama‑3‑1‑70B, Llama‑3‑70B) and their uncoupled fine‑tuned versions.  
* **Additional Benchmarks** – Out‑of‑domain multilingual tasks: MMMLU, MGSM; cross‑lingual benchmarks: LAMBADA (perplexity) and PAWS‑X (accuracy).
