# Summary

## TL;DR  
HIVE is a human‑inspired automatic video‑editing framework that fuses multimodal narrative understanding (character extraction, dialogue analysis, and scene‑level segmentation) with a three‑step editing strategy (highlight detection, opening/ending selection, and pruning).  On the newly released DramaAD benchmark (2.6K short‑drama videos + 522 reference ads), HIVE consistently outperforms existing baselines such as FunClip [1] and AI YouTube Shorts Generator [2], closing the gap to human‑edited clips.

## Problem & Motivation  
The explosion of long‑form content on short‑video platforms creates a high cognitive load for viewers.  Manual editing—watching the entire video and selecting highlights—is labor‑intensive and does not scale.  Existing automatic methods mainly rely on ASR transcripts and end‑to‑end shot selection, ignoring rich visual cues and producing incoherent, abrupt cuts.  The paper argues that a comprehensive multimodal narrative understanding and a systematic, human‑like editing pipeline are needed to generate engaging, coherent clips.

## Approach  
HIVE is a two‑stage pipeline:

1. **Multimodal Narrative Understanding**  
   * **Dialogue Analysis** – ASR transcripts are corrected with OCR subtitles (Bautista & Atienza, 2022) and speaker diarization (Horiguchi et al., 2020).  
   * **Character Extraction** – Face detection (Deng et al., 2019) + clustering + name extraction from dialogues.  
   * **Character‑Dialogue Matching** – Uses Doubao 1.5 pro to align facial images with spoken content.  
   * **Scene Segmentation** – AutoShot (Zhu et al., 2023) for shot boundaries, Qwen2‑VL (Wang et al., 2024b) to merge shots of the same site, and Gemini 2.0 flash to merge shots of the same event, yielding semantically complete scenes.  
   * **Memory Module** – Stores character and narrative information across episodes for efficient retrieval.

2. **Human‑Inspired Editing Framework**  
   * **Highlight Detection** – DeepSeek‑R1 (Guo et al., 2025) evaluates scenes against a rule set (Appendix A.3) and merges adjacent high‑scoring scenes into highlight clips.  
   * **Opening & Ending Selection** – GPT‑4o evaluates candidate openings (preceding general scenes or first scene of a highlight) and endings (following general scenes or last scene of a highlight) (Appendix A.4).  
   * **Content Pruning** – GPT‑4o removes redundant general scenes within the selected boundaries, ensuring a tight narrative flow.  
   * The full algorithm is formalized in Algorithm 1.

The framework is model‑agnostic; any suitable multimodal LLM can replace the current components.

## Experimental Setup  
* **Dataset** – DramaAD (Ours): 3104 videos (2582 short dramas, 522 edited advertisement clips).  Average duration ≈ 40 min, resolution 720P–1080P, aspect ratio 9:16.  Table 1 compares DramaAD to other related datasets.  
* **Baselines** – FunClip [1] and AI YouTube Shorts Generator [2] (both ASR‑based).  The paper also references earlier methods (Chen et al., 2016; Podlesnyy, 2020; Argaw et al., 2022; Hu et al., 2023) as conceptual baselines.  
* **Metrics** – A suite of quantitative metrics covering multiple aspects of editing quality is proposed (details in the supplementary material).  Exact metric names are not listed in the main text.  
* **Evaluation Context** – Offline evaluation on DramaAD; no online deployment or A/B‑testing is reported.

## Results  
* HIVE consistently outperforms all baselines on both general and advertisement‑oriented editing tasks.  
* The paper claims a significant narrowing of the quality gap between automatic and human‑edited videos, but does not provide explicit numeric scores or statistical tests in the main text.  
* No production or A/B‑test results are reported.
