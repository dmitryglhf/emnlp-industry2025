# Summary

## TL;DR  
DetailScribe is a two‑stage refinement framework that first decomposes a text prompt into a hierarchical concept graph using an LLM, then uses a multimodal LLM (GPT‑40) to critique and refine the generated image, and finally re‑denoises the diffusion model to correct only the identified errors. On the newly‑created InterActing dataset of 1,000 LLM‑generated prompts, DetailScribe outperforms Stable Diffusion, GPT‑based prompt rewriting, and DALL·E 3, achieving the highest human‑rated alignment and automatic metric scores, demonstrating a practical approach for industry‑grade text‑to‑image systems that need fine‑grained entity interactions.

## Problem & Motivation  
Text‑to‑image (T2I) models excel at generating single objects or simple scenes, but struggle with intricate interactions between entities—especially when the interactions are uncommon or abstract (e.g., animals using tools, abstract layouts). Existing benchmarks focus on single‑object or simple spatial relations, leaving a gap for evaluating and training models on rich entity interactions. The paper introduces the InterActing dataset to fill this gap and motivates a refinement strategy that can be applied to any pre‑trained T2I model without additional training data.

## Approach  
1. **Concept Decomposition** – An LLM receives the raw prompt and outputs a directed acyclic graph (DAG) where each node is a sub‑concept (entity or interaction) and edges encode dependencies.  
2. **MLLM Critique & Prompt Refinement** – Using GPT‑40, the system reviews the generated image against the DAG, identifies discrepancies, and proposes a refined prompt that adds focused clarifications.  
3. **Diffusion Re‑denoising** – The refined prompt is fed to the same diffusion model (Stable Diffusion SD3.5‑large). Instead of regenerating from scratch, the existing image is partially noised to a chosen time step \(t'\) and the reverse diffusion process is rerun, allowing selective correction while preserving the overall structure.  
The pipeline is compatible with any pre‑trained T2I model and requires no extra training data.

## Experimental Setup  
- **Dataset**: InterActing – 1,000 LLM‑generated prompts split into three scenarios:  
  - Functional & Action‑Based Interactions (600 prompts; Tool Manipulation 227, Physical Contact 373)  
  - Multi‑subject Interactions (200 prompts; Interaction 200)  
  - Compositional Spatial Relationships (200 prompts; Abstract Layouts 183, Spatial Relationships 200, Geometric Patterns 17).  
- **Baselines**:  
  - Stable Diffusion SD3.5‑large (SD)  
  - SD + GPT Rewrite (GPT‑4o generates a detailed prompt from the original)  
  - SD + GPT Refine (GPT‑4o refines the prompt based on the first image)  
  - SD + Inf Scale (toy noise‑searching from Ma et al., 2025)  
  - DALL·E 3 (internal GPT‑based prompt refinement)  
- **Evaluation Metrics**:  
  - Human Likert scale (1–5)  
  - MLLM evaluation (Qwen2.5‑VL‑32B)  
  - ImageReward (ImReward)  
  - CLIPScore (CLIPS.)  
  - BLIP‑VQA (B‑VQA)  
- **Deployment Context**: Not reported.

## Results  
| Model | Functional | Multi‑subject | Compositional | Human | MLLM | ImReward | CLIPS | B‑VQA |
|-------|------------|---------------|----------------|--------|-------|----------|--------|--------|
| SD | 3.360 | 4.480 | 1.657 | 0.971 | 0.366 | 3.225 | 3.800 | 1.171 |
| SD + GPT Rewrite | 3.770 | 4.440 | 1.546 | 0.921 | 0.245 | 3.275 | 3.900 | 1.101 |
| SD + GPT Refine | 3.450 | 4.600 | 1.524 | 0.951 | 0.290 | 3.175 | 3.600 | 1.022 |
| SD + Inf Scale | 3.270 | 4.560 | 1.718 | 0.985 | 0.434 | 3.375 | 4.400 | 1.149 |
| DALL·E 3 | 3.940 | 4.680 | 1.535 | 0.880 | 0.226 | 3.775 | 4.700 | 1.111 |
| **DetailScribe** | **4.280** | **4.880** | **1.761** | **0.998** | **0.449** | **3.800** | **4.600** | **1.326** |

*Interpretation*:  
- DetailScribe improves the human Likert score by **+0.92** over SD and **+0.34** over DALL·E 3.  
- In the MLLM metric, DetailScribe gains **+0.45** over SD and **+0.22** over DALL·E 3.  
- Automatic metrics (ImageReward, CLIPScore, BLIP‑VQA) also show the highest values, with CLIPScore reaching **4.600** (vs 4.400 for SD + Inf Scale).  
- Across all three scenarios, DetailScribe consistently outperforms every baseline, confirming the effectiveness of concept decomposition + MLLM critique + partial re‑denoising.
