# Summary

## TL;DR  
ProFees is an LLM‑driven framework that tackles the real‑world complexities of CPT E/M coding by combining dynamic few‑shot prompting, explicit self‑critique, and self‑consistency voting with a deterministic decision tree. On a proprietary test set of 99 real‑world encounters, ProFees improves exact‑match accuracy by **>36 %** over a commercial CPT E/M system and by **≈5 %** over the best single‑prompt baseline, demonstrating its practical value for production billing pipelines.

## Problem & Motivation  
Accurate CPT E/M coding is essential for billing, compliance, and revenue capture. Manual coding is labor‑intensive, inconsistent, and error‑prone, with studies reporting up to 42 % miscoding and $6.7 B in Medicare overpayments in 2010. Existing automated approaches (classical ML, deep learning, or naïve LLM prompting) struggle with guideline complexity, intermediate MDM levels, and stochastic outputs, limiting their adoption in real‑world EHR systems.

## Approach  
ProFees decomposes the task into two LLM‑based modules:

1. **Encounter Type Classifier** – predicts visit type (inpatient, outpatient, preventive) from SOAP notes, age, and structured EHR data.  
2. **MDM Complexity Classifier** – predicts the 2‑out‑of‑3 MDM level (straightforward, low, moderate, high) using:
   * **Dynamic few‑shot CoT prompting** with a Vector Database (VDB) of 117 annotated encounters (3 MDM elements + justifications).  
   * **Recursive Criticism & Improvement (RCI)** – self‑critics audit each MDM element against checklists.  
   * **Self‑consistency voting** – the RCI‑enhanced classifier is run *K=3* times; majority voting on each element yields deterministic predictions.  

The two predictions feed a **rule‑based decision tree** (mirroring the CPT E/M decision tree) that outputs the final CPT code and justification. This architecture explicitly addresses the five key challenges listed in Table 1: intermediate labels, label noise, explainability, robustness, and clinical breadth.

The test set is a separate 99‑encounter subset, annotated by an internal medical expert in the same manner as the 117‑encounter development set. Evaluation metric: **exact‑match accuracy** (only the final CPT code). Baselines: a commercial CPT E/M coding system (unnamed) and a single‑prompt LLM baseline (unnamed). No online deployment or A/B‑test results are reported.

## Results  
- **ProFees vs. Commercial System** – accuracy improvement **>36 %** (absolute numbers not disclosed).  
- **ProFees vs. Strongest Single‑Prompt Baseline** – accuracy improvement **≈5 %**.  
- No absolute accuracy figures are provided; only relative gains are reported.  
- No production or A/B‑test metrics are reported.

## Industry Impact  
Not reported. The paper states that ProFees was developed and deployed within a production EHR system, but does not disclose the deploying organization, scale, product, or business outcomes.

## Limitations  
- **Label noise**: External and internal experts disagree on 56 % of encounters.  
- **Stochasticity**: LLM outputs vary; mitigated by self‑consistency voting but still a concern.  
- **Intermediate labels**: Production data lack MDM element annotations; addressed by re‑annotating 117 encounters.  
- **Explainability**: While chain‑of‑thought and critic outputs are provided, the system still relies on LLM reasoning, which may be opaque to auditors.  
- **Generalizability**: The approach is evaluated only on outpatient/preventive encounters; performance on other visit types is not reported.
