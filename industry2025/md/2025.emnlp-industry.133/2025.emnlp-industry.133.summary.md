# Summary

## TL;DR
The paper introduces **Mind the Query**, a high‑quality, multi‑domain benchmark for the *Text2Cypher* task. It contains 27,529 natural‑language to Cypher query pairs across 11 real‑world graph datasets, each with an executable graph database and rigorous automated + manual validation. The dataset enables fine‑grained evaluation of large language models (LLMs) and supports future research on reasoning‑based Text2Cypher systems.

## Problem & Motivation
- **Real‑world problem**: Translating natural‑language questions into executable Cypher queries over graph databases is essential for democratizing graph analytics but remains error‑prone due to schema, syntax, and semantic misalignments.
- **Existing gaps**:
  - Prior datasets (Neo4j Text2Cypher, SyntheT2C) lack human verification, often contain syntactic or semantic errors, and omit the underlying graph databases.
  - Domain coverage is limited (e.g., SyntheT2C focuses on medicine).
- **Industry relevance**: Accurate Text2Cypher enables non‑technical users to query complex graph data in domains such as healthcare, transportation, and social media, directly impacting business analytics pipelines.

## Approach
- **End‑to‑end synthetic pipeline**:
  1. **LLM generation**: Uses Gemini Flash 2.0 to produce *NL‑Cypher* pairs guided by schema‑aware prompts.
  2. **Automatic validation**: Three validators—Schema, Runtime, Value—filter syntactic and semantic errors.
  3. **Human verification**: Two annotators review logical correctness; a third arbitrates disagreements.
- **Query categories** (used for generation and evaluation):
  - Simple Retrieval (SR)
  - Complex Retrieval (CR)
  - Simple Aggregation (SA)
  - Complex Aggregation (CA)
  - Evaluation Queries (EQ)
- **Prompt engineering**: Four setups—Zero‑shot, Zero‑shot + Instruction, Few‑shot, Few‑shot + Instruction—showed that instruction‑guided prompts with few‑shot examples yield the best syntactic/semantic alignment.
- **Few‑shot curation**: 10 manually curated examples per category; cross‑fewshots used for datasets lacking category‑specific examples.
- **Dataset coverage**: 11 real‑world KGs—Healthcare Analytics (HCA), Open Street Map (OSM), Entity Resolution (ER), Women World Cup 2019 (WWC), Legis Graph (LG), Contact Tracing (CT), Bloom, Graph Data Science (GDSC), Pole, Star Wars (SW), US Election Twitter Trolls (USTT).

## Experimental Setup
- **Dataset split**: 18,470 train (67.09 %), 2,000 validation (7.26 %), 7,059 test (24.59 %). Test set contains manually verified queries from WWC, HCA, and Bloom; the remaining 8 datasets form train/validation.
- **Models evaluated**: 10 state‑of‑the‑art LLMs:
  - Granite‑3.3‑8B‑Instruct
  - Granite‑34B‑Code‑Instruct
  - Llama‑4‑Maverick‑17B‑128E‑Instruct
  - Llama‑3.3‑70B‑Instruct
  - Llama‑3.1‑8B‑Instruct
  - Mixtral‑8x22B
  - DeepSeek‑Coder‑33B‑Instruct
  - DeepSeek‑V3
  - Phi‑4
  - Qwen2.5‑72B‑Instruct
- **Experimental paradigms**: Zero‑shot, Few‑shot, and Fine‑tuned (not detailed in the paper).
- **Evaluation metric**: Execution accuracy (percentage of queries that execute correctly on the provided graph database).

## Results
- **Validation statistics**:
  - 4,829 automatically validated *NL‑Cypher* pairs.
  - 4,696 of them (97.2 %) passed manual logical correctness checks.
- **Query distribution** (Table 1):
  - CA: 5,695
  - CR: 5,196
  - EQ: 6,718
  - SA: 3,719
  - SR: 6,201
  - Total: 27,529
- **Model performance**: The paper presents a comparison table (Table 3) for zero‑shot and few‑shot settings, but specific accuracy numbers are omitted in the provided excerpt. The authors note that instruction‑guided prompts with few‑shot examples yield the best results across all query categories.
- **Common error patterns** (manual review):
  - Structural hallucination (e.g., confusing `PLAYED_IN` vs. `PLAYED_FOR`).
  - Aggregator hallucination (e.g., inserting SQL‑style `INTERSECTION`, `SUM(... ) OVER (…)`).

## Industry Impact
Not reported.

## Limitations
- **Residual errors**: Despite 97.2 % logical correctness, some queries still return empty results due to missing data or subtle schema mismatches.
- **Domain separation**: Strict train/test separation may limit evaluation on truly unseen schemas.
- **Manual effort**: Human verification is required for logical correctness; scaling beyond the current dataset would need additional annotators.
- **Model dependence**: The generation pipeline relies on Gemini Flash 2.0; performance may vary with other LLMs or prompt designs.
