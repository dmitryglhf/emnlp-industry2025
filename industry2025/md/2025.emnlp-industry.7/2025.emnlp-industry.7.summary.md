# Summary

## TL;DR  
The paper introduces an end‑to‑end audio chatbot that routes user queries to specialized audio expert models (ASR, speaker diarization, music identification, text‑to‑audio) using a BERT‑based intent classifier, then fuses the expert outputs with an Audio Context Detection (ACD) module and a 3.8 B Phi‑3.5 LLM to generate natural‑language responses. The system outperforms LLM‑fewshot intent classifiers and state‑of‑the‑art large audio language models on custom temporal‑reasoning benchmarks (ACD‑timestamp‑QA and ACD‑temporal‑QA) and on the MMAU sound test set, making it suitable for on‑device deployment.

## Problem & Motivation  
Existing chatbots (LUIS, DialogFlow, Lex) and multimodal LLMs focus mainly on speech or text, leaving a wide range of audio‑related queries (e.g., music identification, speaker diarization, timestamp reasoning) largely unaddressed. The industry demands a single system that can understand and answer diverse audio queries in real‑world contexts, but current solutions either lack breadth or are too heavy for edge devices.

## Approach  
1. **Intent Classification** – A BERT‑based classifier is trained on a newly created **Audio‑Intent** dataset (12,661 samples, 8 intents) to route queries to the appropriate expert model.  
2. **Expert Models** –  
   * **ASR**: Whisper (Radford et al., 2023)  
   * **Speaker Diarization**: Pyannote (Bredin, 2023)  
   * **Music Identification**: ACRCloud (ACRCloud, 2024)  
   * **Text‑to‑Audio**: Huang et al., 2023  
   * **Audio Context Detection (ACD)**: CNN10‑PANN (Kong et al., 2020) to extract event names, start/end times, and durations.  
3. **Response Generation** – A 3.8 B Phi‑3.5 LLM receives the expert outputs, chat history (last 10 turns), and ACD metadata (JSON format) to produce the final answer.  
4. **Fallback** – If experts fail, an AQA‑LLM (Sridhar et al., 2024) generates a generic reply.  
5. **New Benchmarks** – Two custom datasets:  
   * **ACD‑timestamp‑QA** (960 entries, GPT‑4 augmented) – tests timestamp extraction.  
   * **ACD‑temporal‑QA** (1,500 entries, GPT‑4 augmented) – tests chronological reasoning.

## Experimental Setup  
* **Datasets** – Audio‑Intent (training 10,328 / test 2,333), ACD‑timestamp‑QA (960), ACD‑temporal‑QA (1,500), MMAU sound test set.  
* **Baselines** – BERT‑intent classifier vs. LLM‑fewshot (Phi‑3.5). For audio tasks, compared against state‑of‑the‑art large audio language models (7 B‑parameter range).  
* **Metrics** – Precision, Recall, F1 for intent classification; overall accuracy; qualitative accuracy on custom tasks and MMAU.  
* **Deployment Context** – Designed for on‑device inference; 3.8 B Phi‑3.5 chosen for edge feasibility.

## Results  
| Intent | BERT Precision | BERT Recall | BERT F1 | LLM Precision | LLM Recall | LLM F1 |
|--------|----------------|-------------|---------|----------------|------------|--------|
| Music identification | 0.98 | 1.00 | 0.99 | 0.99 | 0.63 | 0.72 |
| Music recommendation | 0.80 | 0.91 | 0.85 | 0.88 | 0.61 | 0.72 |
| ASR whisper | 0.86 | 0.86 | 0.86 | 0.86 | 0.36 | 0.43 |
| Speaker ID, Diarization, counting | 0.97 | 0.87 | 0.92 | 0.72 | 0.68 | 0.70 |
| Audio/Text to Audio | 0.95 | 1.00 | 0.97 | 0.86 | 0.37 | 0.52 |
| Source separation/removal | 0.98 | 0.74 | 0.84 | 0.20 | 0.89 | 0.32 |
| LLM | 0.87 | 0.88 | 0.88 | 0.09 | 0.09 | 0.09 |
| Unsupported | 0.84 | 0.83 | 0.85 | 0.18 | 0.17 | 0.18 |
| **Overall accuracy** | – | 0.90 | – | – | 0.37 | – |

* The BERT classifier achieves **90 %** overall accuracy versus **37 %** for the LLM‑fewshot approach.  
* On the custom ACD‑timestamp‑QA and ACD‑temporal‑QA datasets, the system significantly outperforms baseline audio‑language models (exact numbers not reported).  
* On the MMAU sound test set, the system outperforms all 7 B‑parameter models, demonstrating its suitability for on‑device deployment.

## Industry Impact  
Not reported.

## Limitations  
* LLM‑based intent classification is resource‑intensive and unsuitable for real‑world edge deployment.  
* The study does not provide large‑scale deployment or A/B‑testing results.  
* No explicit discussion of latency or memory footprint beyond the 3.8 B Phi‑3.5 model choice.
