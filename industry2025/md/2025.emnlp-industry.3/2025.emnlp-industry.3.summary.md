# Summary

## TL;DR  
CRAB is the first multilingual benchmark that evaluates the *curation* ability of Retrieval‑Augmented LLMs in biomedicine.  It provides 400 open‑ended queries (English, French, German, Chinese) with manually curated relevant/irrelevant reference sets (2 467 relevant, 1 854 irrelevant) and a citation‑based metric (RP, IS, CE).  Experiments show large gaps among mainstream LLMs (e.g., GPT‑4o CE 80.46 in Chinese, o3‑mini CE 71.47 in German, Gemini‑1.5‑pro CE 71.91 in English, Doubao CE 73.07 in French) and reveal that reasoning‑focused models do not improve curation.

## Problem & Motivation  
Retrieval‑Augmented Generation (RAG) mitigates hallucinations but introduces *curation* challenges: LLMs may cite irrelevant or incorrect references, especially in biomedicine where evidence‑based citations are critical.  Existing benchmarks focus on answer correctness and ignore whether the model properly selects and cites references.  CRAB addresses this gap by evaluating curation in a multilingual, open‑ended setting.

## Approach  
1. **Data construction** – 400 queries collected from experts across five biomedical categories (Basic Biology, Drug Development & Design, Clinical Translation & Application, Ethics & Regulation, Public Health & Infectious Disease).  
2. **Reference retrieval** – LlamaIndex retrieves all available references from PubMed and Google; top‑20 from each source are combined.  Experts label retrieved items as *relevant* or *irrelevant*.  Irrelevant references are further enriched by reconstructing queries (e.g., “mechanism of action of Oseltamivir” → “indications of Oseltamivir”).  
3. **Evaluation metric** – Citation‑based verification: for each reference in the generated answer, the model’s prediction (cite/not cite) is compared to the ground‑truth label.  Metrics: Relevance Precision (RP), Irrelevance Suppression (IS), and overall Curation Efficiency (CE) = macro‑average F1.

## Experimental Setup  
- **Models** – Closed‑source: GPT‑4o, GPT‑5, Claude‑3.7‑sonnet, Gemini‑1.5‑pro, Gemini‑2.5‑pro; Open‑source: DeepSeek‑V3, DeepSeek‑R1, Doubao, QwQ, o3‑mini.  
- **Inference** – Official recommended hyper‑parameters; random seed 42; ratio of relevant:irrelevant references fixed at 2:3; reference order shuffled.  
- **Languages** – English, French, German, Chinese.  
- **Human evaluation** – Biomedical experts verify whether cited content is generated from the corresponding reference.

*Key observations*  
- Closed‑source models (GPT‑4o, GPT‑5, Gemini‑1.5‑pro, Gemini‑2.5‑pro) consistently outperform open‑source models.  
- GPT‑4o achieves the highest CE (80.46) in Chinese; o3‑mini achieves the highest CE (72.98) in German; Gemini‑1.5‑pro achieves the highest CE (71.91) in English; Doubao achieves the highest CE (73.07) in French.  
- Reasoning LLMs (o3‑mini, Gemini‑2.5‑pro, DeepSeek‑R1, QwQ) do not improve curation compared to their base models; some even decline in English and Chinese.  
- Some models exhibit bias toward RP or CE (e.g., Doubao has high CE but low RP).
