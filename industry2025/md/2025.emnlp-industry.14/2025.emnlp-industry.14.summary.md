# Summary

## TL;DR  
The paper introduces **Patch Context Robustness Index (PCRI)**, a new score that measures how much Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) rely on global visual context versus local patches. Across 19 recent MLLMs and 15 vision‑language benchmarks, most models show *negative* PCRI values—performing better on isolated patches than on full images—indicating brittleness to background noise. Only a few large models (e.g., **InternVL2‑26B** and **Qwen2VL‑72B**) achieve near‑zero PCRI, suggesting robust handling of visual context. PCRI therefore offers a principled, interpretable metric for model selection and diagnostic analysis in real‑world deployments.

## Problem & Motivation  
MLLMs are increasingly used in enterprise settings such as visual question answering, e‑commerce product search, and assistive document understanding. In practice, images often contain clutter, occlusions, or irrelevant backgrounds that can mislead models. Existing benchmarks evaluate models on full‑image inputs, implicitly assuming all visual regions are equally relevant. This assumption rarely holds, leading to failures when models encounter noisy or dynamic visual environments. The authors argue that practitioners need a quantitative tool to assess *context robustness*—the ability to maintain performance when visual granularity changes—yet no such metric exists.

## Approach  
The authors propose **PCRI**, defined as  

\[
\text{PCRI}_n = 1 - \frac{P_{\text{patch},n}}{P_{\text{whole}}}
\]

where \(P_{\text{whole}}\) is the model’s performance on the full image, and \(P_{\text{patch},n}\) is the maximum performance over all \(n\times n\) non‑overlapping patches.  
Key design choices:

* **Patch‑based evaluation**: Images are split into a regular grid (granularities \(n=2\) and \(n=3\)), and the model is evaluated independently on each patch.  
* **Max aggregation**: The best patch score is compared to the whole‑image score, highlighting whether global context helps or hurts.  
* **Validity gate**: PCRI is only reported when the full‑image score is meaningfully above chance (dataset‑specific chance floor + margin).  

The framework is simple, reproducible, and applicable to any MLLM and vision‑language task.

## Experimental Setup  
* **Datasets** (15 vision‑language benchmarks):  
  * Image Captioning – **MS‑COCO Captions**  
  * Multiple‑Choice QA – **AI2D, BLINK, MMMU, MMStar, RealWorldQA, ScienceQA**  
  * Yes/No Classification – **AMBER, Hallusion‑Bench, MME, POPE**  
  * Visual Question Answering – **ChartQA, GQA, TextVQA, VizWiz**  

* **Models** (19 state‑of‑the‑art MLLMs):  
  * **InternVL** variants (including InternVL2‑26B)  
  * **Qwen2VL** variants (including Qwen2VL‑72B)  
  * **Molmo**  
  * **NVLM**  
  * **Pixtral**  
  * **Janus‑Pro‑7B**  
  * Others (not all named in the paper).  

* **Granularities**: \(n=2\) (4 patches) and \(n=3\) (9 patches).  
* **Evaluation**: All runs use **VLMEvalKit**; compute cost scales with \(n^2\) relative to a single full‑image pass.  
* **Metrics**: PCRI is agnostic to the underlying task metric (accuracy, BLEU, etc.) and is interpreted relative to dataset‑specific chance.

## Results  
* **Overall trend**: 19 MLLMs exhibit *negative* PCRI values, meaning they perform better on localized patches than on full images.  
* **Model‑specific findings**:  
  * **InternVL2‑26B** and **Qwen2VL‑72B** have near‑zero PCRI, indicating robust handling of global context.  
  * Smaller InternVL variants, **NVLM**, and **Pixtral** show the lowest PCRI values (most negative), suggesting high sensitivity to background noise.  
* **Task‑type effects**:  
  * **Captioning** (MS‑COCO) shows the strongest negative PCRI, e.g., InternVL2‑2B reaches –0.49 at \(2\times2\) and –0.75 at \(3\times3\).  
  * **Multiple‑Choice QA** shows moderate negative PCRI; larger models (Qwen2‑VL‑72B) are more robust.  
  * **Yes/No** tasks have the mildest PCRI, indicating simpler visual reasoning.  
* **Granularity impact**: Increasing from \(2\times2\) to \(3\times3\) consistently amplifies negative PCRI across tasks, but larger models (26B, 72B) exhibit smaller shifts, reflecting better global‑context integration.  
* **Human comparison**: In a human study, annotators never outperformed full‑image conditions, contrasting with MLLMs that sometimes benefit from patches—highlighting a deviation from human‑like contextual reasoning.
