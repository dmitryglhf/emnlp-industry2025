# Summary

## TL;DR  
The paper presents a unified SpeechLLM architecture that jointly processes speech and text to perform zero‑shot slot filling on large‑scale call‑center data. By carefully designing modality adapters, employing multi‑task alignment, and multi‑stage fine‑tuning, the authors close the performance gap to a cascaded baseline while enabling generalization to unseen slot labels—an essential capability for industry deployments that require rapid domain adaptation.

## Problem & Motivation  
- **Real‑world problem**: Slot filling is a core subtask in spoken language understanding (SLU) for goal‑oriented conversational systems, especially in call‑center domains (banking, telecommunication, insurance, retail).  
- **Existing solutions fall short**: Traditional cascaded ASR + NLU pipelines are limited to predefined closed‑domain slot sets, suffer from error propagation, and require costly re‑engineering for new domains or evolving slot types.  
- **Industry context**: Rapid introduction of new intents and slot types in call‑center operations demands a model that can generalize to unseen slot labels without additional training data.

## Approach  
- **Core technical idea**: A SpeechLLM architecture comprising a pretrained speech encoder, a modality adapter, and a decoder‑only large language model (LLM).  
- **Key components**:  
  1. **Speech encoder** – pretrained on web‑scale speech data.  
  2. **Modality adapter** – transforms encoder outputs into a representation compatible with the LLM’s linguistic input space.  
  3. **LLM** – Llama 3.2 1B, fine‑tuned with instruction‑based prompts.  
- **Notable design choices**:  
  - Use of GPT‑4o to annotate the CallCenter‑A dataset with slot labels and values.  
  - Instruction‑based training dataset with diverse prompts (10 prompt templates) and random context sizes (0–3 turns) and distractors (1–5).  
  - Multi‑task datasets: AST (internal), SIT and SQIT (spoken Alpaca converted via text‑to‑speech).  
  - Multi‑stage training strategies:  
    - **Multistage‑A**: Stage 1‑a fine‑tune Whisper encoder, Stage 1‑b fine‑tune LLM, Stage 2 joint fine‑tune adapter + LLM.  
    - **Multistage‑B**: Stage 1 align adapter via “continuation” task, Stage 2 joint fine‑tune.  
    - **Multistage‑C**: Stage 1 align adapter via AST task, Stage 2 joint fine‑tune.  
  - Parameter‑efficient fine‑tuning (PEFT) with QLoRA (4‑bit quantization, LoRA rank 32, α = 128, dropout 0.05).  
  - Adapter architectures explored: CNN, Linear, MLP, Transformer, and a larger CNN‑XL.

## Experimental Setup  
- **Datasets**:  
  - **CallCenter‑A**: ~31 K calls, ~1 M turns, 2.1 K hours (banking, telecom, insurance, retail).  
  - **CallCenter‑B**: 80 calls, 4 495 turns (split into ID/OOD).  
  - **Internal AST**: subset of CallCenter‑A for acoustic quality.  
  - **Spoken Alpaca**: SIT (30 samples) and SQIT (14 samples).  
- **Baselines**:  
  1. **Vanilla LLM** – Llama 3.2 1B, no fine‑tuning.  
  2. **FT‑LLM** – fine‑tuned Llama 3.2 1B on CallCenter‑A text.  
  3. **Whisper FT‑LLM** – Whisper‑base ASR fine‑tuned on internal AST, followed by FT‑LLM.  
  4. **SpeechLLM (baseline)** – CNN adapter only.  
- **Evaluation metrics**: partial‑match precision/recall/F1.  
- **Training details**: NVIDIA A10G GPUs, batch 128 (4 per GPU, accumulation 8), cosine LR schedule (max ×10⁻⁴, 20 % warm‑up), 10–15 epochs, AdamW, gradient clipping 1.0.  
- **Whisper FT‑LLM WER**: 14.20 %.  

## Results  
| System | CallCenter‑A Prec / Recall / F1 |
|--------|--------------------------------|
| Vanilla LLM | 0.0436 / 0.7259 / 0.0823 |
| FT‑LLM | 0.8523 / 0.9901 / 0.9160 |
| Whisper FT‑LLM | 0.7006 / 0.8982 / 0.7872 |
| SpeechLLM (baseline) | 0.3892 / 0.4151 / 0.4017 |

**Out‑of‑domain performance (Table 6)**  
- Cascaded system (Whisper FT‑LLM) remains more robust, generalizes better, and shows stronger zero‑shot capabilities than the SpeechLLM architecture, indicating that joint modality alignment alone is insufficient without larger scale or data.
