# Summary

## TL;DR
RAVEN++ extends the RAVEN video‑moderation framework with **active reinforcement learning**, a **hierarchical reward design**, and a **progressive multi‑stage training** pipeline. The result is a model that outperforms both general‑purpose LLMs (LLaVA, Qwen‑2.5‑VL‑7B) and the original RAVEN on fine‑grained violation detection, sub‑category classification, and temporal grounding, achieving up to **+4.4 % absolute precision** on major categories and **+0.6 % mIoU** on sub‑scene localization.

## Problem & Motivation
Video advertisements are legally regulated but hard to moderate because violations can be subtle, temporally distributed, and require precise localization. Existing systems, including RAVEN, struggle with:
- **Fine‑grained understanding** (sub‑category and temporal precision),
- **Explainability** (verbose or contradictory chain‑of‑thoughts),
- **Generalization** to out‑of‑domain data.

These gaps limit industrial deployment where compliance and user trust are critical.

## Approach
RAVEN++ introduces three core innovations:

1. **Active Reinforcement Learning (RL)**  
   * Dynamically selects samples of varying difficulty during training.  
   * Interleaves supervised fine‑tuning (SFT) with RL to balance knowledge acquisition and refinement.

2. **Fine‑Grained Violation Understanding**  
   * Six reward components: format, violation, major/sub‑category (Tversky distance), temporal grounding (IoU + boundary), and reasoning consistency (semantic cosine with Qwen‑72B).  
   * Rewards are weighted by λ‑parameters that shift across three curriculum phases.

3. **Progressive Multi‑Stage Training**  
   * **Stage 1 – Knowledge Injection**: Joint SFT on QA pairs generated by Qwen‑72B (product, audience, rule hierarchy).  
   * **Stage 2 – Passive RL**: Curriculum‑based passive RL on large‑scale coarsely annotated data plus a small precisely annotated set.  
   * **Stage 3 – Active RL**: Targeted replay on a small high‑quality annotated set, with dynamic buffer assignment (fundamental gaps → SFT, refinement → RL, standard → low‑weight RL).

## Experimental Setup
| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|

Training uses a small learning rate during the active RL phase to avoid over‑fitting.

## Results
| Metric | RAVEN | RAVEN++ |
|--------|-------|---------|
| **Major Category (P/R)** | 0.851/0.801 | **0.905/0.840** |
| **Sub‑Category (P/R)** | 0.638/0.585 | **0.735/0.654** |
| **Grounding (mIoU)** | 0.521 | **0.601** |
| **Sub‑Category (P/R) Avg** | 0.602/0.586 | **0.690/0.650** |

*Table 1* (major category & grounding) and *Table 2* (sub‑category) show that RAVEN++ consistently outperforms RAVEN and the LLM baselines across all metrics. The improvements are most pronounced on the hardest tasks: sub‑category classification and temporal grounding.
