# Summary

## TL;DR
PolyNorm is a prompt‑driven, few‑shot text‑normalization framework that replaces hand‑crafted rule systems with large language models (LLMs). Across eight languages and 27 normalization categories, PolyNorm reduces word‑error rates (WER) by 4–10 % relative to a production‑grade rule‑based baseline, while achieving BLEU scores above 80 % for GPT‑4o. The authors release a multilingual benchmark (PolyNorm‑Benchmark) and demonstrate that LLM prompting can scale to low‑resource languages with minimal human effort.

## Problem & Motivation
Text Normalization (TN) converts raw text into fluent, speech‑ready forms. Existing rule‑based TN systems are accurate but require extensive manual rule engineering, costly maintenance, and struggle with low‑resource or morphologically rich languages (e.g., Arabic, Polish). They also struggle with contextual ambiguities that depend on language and target domain. The paper argues that LLMs, with their broad linguistic knowledge, can alleviate these issues by learning from few‑shot examples rather than exhaustive rule sets.

## Approach
PolyNorm adopts a three‑component prompt:  
1. **Instruction Prompt** – a language‑agnostic description of the TN task.  
2. **In‑Context Learning (ICL) Examples** – 80–100 high‑quality, language‑specific examples (derived from Kestrel and DeepSeek‑R1) that illustrate each of the 27 categories.  
3. **Target Input** – the unnormalized text to be normalized.  

The system uses GPT‑4o or GPT‑4o‑mini as the underlying LLM. ICL examples are curated by language experts and iteratively refined (hill‑climbing) to target systematic error patterns. The prompt format is identical across eight languages (American English, German, French, Mexican Spanish, Italian, Lithuanian, Mandarin Chinese, Japanese) with only the localized examples changing.

## Experimental Setup
- **Datasets**  
  - *PolyNorm‑Benchmark*: 540 high‑quality pairs per language (27 categories × 20 examples).  
  - *Kestrel* (English): 880 k lines; a balanced subset of 1 400 test cases (100 per category) used for benchmarking.  
  - *DeepSeek‑R1* (2025) used to generate synthetic pairs; each example is then verified by internal experts.  
- **Baselines**  
  - Apple Siri production rule‑based TN system (proprietary).  
- **Systems**  
  - GPT‑4o‑mini  
  - GPT‑4o  
- **Metrics**  
  - Word Error Rate (WER) – token‑level accuracy.  
  - BLEU – surface similarity to reference.  
  - Character Error Rate (CER) for Chinese/Japanese to avoid tokenization artifacts.  
- **Evaluation**  
  - Offline, offline‑style evaluation on the benchmark; no online A/B‑test results reported.

## Limitations
- Performance is highly dependent on the quality and representativeness of the ICL examples; unseen patterns may still fail.  
- The system may propagate errors into downstream tasks (e.g., translation, sentiment analysis).  
- The benchmark and evaluation focus on conventional spoken forms; variations that do not affect pronunciation (e.g., casing, optional spacing) are ignored.  
- The approach requires expert‑verified examples for each language, which may limit rapid deployment in truly low‑resource settings.  
- The authors note that diacritization, non‑whitespace tokenization, and suprasegmental features (pitch, tone) are future work, indicating current limitations in handling such phenomena.
