# Summary

## TL;DR
CitySim is a large‑scale urban simulation framework that uses GPT‑4o‑mini powered agents to generate realistic daily schedules via recursive value‑driven planning, belief‑aware place selection, and dynamic memory. It outperforms baselines on macro‑level time‑use, pairwise human preference, travel patterns, and POI popularity prediction in Tokyo, demonstrating its potential as a scalable, flexible testbed for urban planning.

## Problem & Motivation
Simulating realistic city‑scale human behavior is crucial for social science, behavioral studies, and urban planning. Traditional hand‑crafted rule‑based models lack behavioral diversity, adaptability, and long‑term dynamics. Existing LLM‑based agents still plan myopically, ignore belief updates, and neglect place and vehicle choice. CitySim addresses these gaps by leveraging LLMs to generate realistic daily schedules, beliefs, and long‑term goals.

## Approach
CitySim agents are equipped with:

- **Persona module**: demographic attributes, spatial anchors, Big‑Five psychographic traits, habits and preferences.  
- **Memory module**: temporal, reflective, and spatial memories. Spatial beliefs \(b_i\) are updated via a Kalman filter and decay \(\lambda\).  
- **Belief module**: generates subjective observations via LLM and integrates them with prior beliefs.  
- **Needs module**: tracks hunger, energy, safety, and social needs, initialized by LLM and decayed over time.  
- **Long‑term goal module**: uses Maslow hierarchy and LLM prompts to generate short‑ and long‑term goals.  
- **Perception module**: selects appropriate action modules via LLM.  
- **Planning module**: recursively decomposes the day into blocks, fills mandatory and medium‑priority tasks, and uses value‑driven planning for empty blocks.  
- **Place selection module**: belief‑aware gravity model (Equation (1)) for macro‑level area and micro‑level POI selection.  
- **Vehicle selection module**: LLM prompt to choose transport mode.  
- **Social module**: weighted social network with beliefs \(b_{u,v}\) updated after interactions.

## Experimental Setup
- **Setting**: 1,000 agents in the Tokyo metropolitan area, each powered by GPT‑4o‑mini (except when specified).  
- **Baselines**: GeAn, AGA, HumanoidAgent, MobileCity, AgentSociety.  
- **Evaluation metrics**:  
  - Macro‑level time‑use distribution vs. 2021 Japanese national time‑use survey.  
  - Pairwise human preference: 15 trials per approach, outputs normalized with Llama‑3.1 70B, judged by GPT‑4o on naturalness, coherence, plausibility.  
  - Travel patterns: average number of travels per hour (weekday/weekend) compared to proprietary city‑scale dataset.  
  - POI popularity prediction: real‑world POI popularity in Shibuya (Tokyo) estimated from Google Maps ratings.

## Results
- **Macro‑level time use**: CitySim’s distribution closely matches the 2021 Japanese national time‑use survey (Figure 2).  
- **Pairwise human preferences**: CitySim achieves the highest average win rates in the pairwise win‑rate matrix (Figure 3), outperforming all baselines.  
- **Travel patterns**: CitySim reproduces real‑world commuting peaks and weekend leisure activity (Figure 4). MobileCity shows overly rigid spikes; other baselines are static or mistimed.  
- **POI popularity**: CitySim’s simulated visits correlate well with Google Maps ratings for Shibuya POIs (Figure 5).
